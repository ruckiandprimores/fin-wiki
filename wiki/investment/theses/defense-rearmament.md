# Defense Re-armament — Structural Capex Trend (Active CoC-Build Vertical)

> **TL;DR**: NATO's 5%-by-2035 commitment + contractual multi-year order books make defense the
> **cleanest structural-capex trend in the tape** — flagged independently by the macro, REIT, and
> best-seller research arms. The cycle is **early-to-mid** (spending earlier than price). The thesis
> is durable; the live hazard is **valuation**, not trend — EU pure-plays at 30–40×. Given a
> still-building circle of competence, the natural way to *express* the trend is **ETF-first** (per the
> execution-vehicle typology), rather than concentrating into the momentum names. *Analysis of the
> trend and its valuation hazard — not a recommendation.*

> **Provenance**: the 2026-06-27 sweep + the 2026-06-29 scope revisit that moved defense from a prior
> skip to an active research vertical. Snapshots are late-June 2026 web-sourced — verify before acting.
> *How* the exposure is best expressed (ETF-first for a still-building CoC) is a methodology question,
> not a call — see the [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|execution-vehicle typology]].

## The trend (durable)

- **NATO 5%-of-GDP by 2035** + national procurement commitments → a contractual, multi-year demand
  signal, not a sentiment spike.
- **Order books are contractual and multi-year** — revenue visibility is unusually high for a
  cyclical.
- **Cycle position: early-to-mid** — spending is ramping earlier than equity prices in several
  pockets, but the momentum leaders already price years of growth.

## The hazard (valuation, not trend)

The risk is **paying up**, not the trend failing. EU pure-plays (Rheinmetall, Saab, Thales) trade at
30–40× — they price a structural re-rating that may or may not hold. The unresolved question:
**structural re-rating vs mean-reversion.** If the duration-bulls are right, today's 35× RHM could
prove cheap; if not, it's a momentum top. That tension is why activation leans ETF-first.

## Relative value (the lower-multiple names)

- **Under-priced EU exposure:** BAE Systems (~16–17×) + Leonardo (~18–20×) — cheapest large EU primes
  with EU-rearmament leverage.
- **US value:** Lockheed (~16.8×, cheapest US prime), General Dynamics (~21×), Northrop (~18–23×,
  B-21 / nuclear backlog).
- **Stretched (priced for years of growth):** Rheinmetall (30–39×), Saab (~40×), Thales (~29×).
- **Not a clean play:** Boeing (40×+, unstable) — commercial-aero contamination.

## How the exposure is naturally expressed (ETF-first)

Per the [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|role-(a) logic]] (weak CoC → higher ETF
share), a still-building competence points to an **ETF-first** expression: a diversified NATO wrapper
(HANetf Future of Defence) or a Europe pure-play (WisdomTree Europe Defence) is the natural vehicle,
with **BAE + Leonardo the value-coherent direct names**. ETFs here read as role-(c) concentration
overlays on top of an existing book, or a role-(a) core if defense is held standalone.

**Counter (logged):** if the duration-bulls are right, an ETF-first expression under-captures the
leaders — but concentrating into momentum names at 35× is the larger risk while CoC is thin.

## Falsifier / invalidation

- NATO / national budgets walk back the multi-year commitments (the trend's foundation).
- Order-book cancellations or push-outs appear in prime backlogs (visibility was the edge).
- The valuation premium compresses *because* growth disappoints, not just multiple mean-reversion —
  i.e. the re-rating was unearned.
- **Procurement-reform / attritable-systems redirection (added 2026-07-12, per the Kiel sources): spending stays, beneficiaries change.** The surge currently flows ~90–95% to legacy crewed platforms (confirming for incumbent primes), but the live EU reform agenda — procurement centralisation (~28% measured savings), capacity contracts, R&D share 2%→10%, Ukraine-template attritable systems — redistributes the same money away from legacy-platform primes if adopted. Watch reform adoption, not just budget totals. Structural detail: [[foundation/asset-classes/defense-economics-primer]] §What to Watch.
