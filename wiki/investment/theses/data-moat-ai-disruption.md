# Data-Moat Incumbents vs Generative AI — Durable or Impaired?

> **TL;DR**: Generative AI threatens the **workflow / pricing layer** of professional-data
> incumbents, **not the data / regulatory core.** The Feb-3 2026 AI-legal-tool launch repriced the
> whole complex (RELX −14%, steepest since 1988) — a sentiment-driven de-rate on names whose core
> is mostly AI-immune = a [[foundation/market-wisdom/mr-market|Mr. Market]] setup. Deploy into the
> core-weighted de-rates (**SPGI > RELX > MCO**), size the workflow-wrapper name (TRI) smaller /
> contingent, treat VRSK as a trough-confirmation play, and **drop EQIX** (mis-bucketed). This is
> dated analysis exposing the deployment reasoning — not advice, and not a sizing instruction.

## The thesis question

Does generative AI break the professional-data moat? Both the attack and defense arms converge on
one dividing line — **commoditizable wrapper** (legal research, document review, research
terminals) **vs proprietary-dataset-is-the-product vs regulatory franchise.** AI is a *customer* of
the latter two and a *competitor* of the first.

## Two corrections that defuse the scare (attack arm)

1. **The "$143B under reconsideration" is mis-read.** It comes from TRI's *own* "Future of
   Professionals" report = law/accounting **firms' client revenue** at risk (a sales argument for
   adopting TRI's AI). It is **not** TRI's own revenue at risk — a magnitude-of-disruption proxy,
   not incumbent churn.
2. **SPGI's "guidance cut" was FX-driven, not AI.** SPGI **maintained** 6–8% organic cc guidance;
   only reported revenue trimmed ~30bps on FX. Market Intelligence is the *slowest* segment (+8% vs
   Ratings +13% / Indices +17%) with analyst AI-anxiety attached — but no disclosed AI-driven cut.
   (Corrects the earlier SPGI falsifier wording.)

## The market event (frames the de-rate)

**Feb 3, 2026**: Anthropic Claude Cowork legal plugins → TRI −18%, **RELX −14% (steepest since
1988)**, Wolters Kluwer −13%, LSEG −8%. One product launch repriced the whole complex → a
sentiment-driven de-rate on names whose core is mostly AI-immune.

## Two rankings (near-mirror images, coherent)

- **Attack-surface (most exposed → least):** TRI > RELX > SPGI > VRSK > MCO
- **Moat-defensibility (most → least):** SPGI > MCO > RELX > TRI > VRSK
- **Structural AI-immune % of revenue:** CME ~85% · MCO ~55% · SPGI ~50% (Ratings 35% + Indices 14%)

## Falsifiers (per name)

- **SPGI:** MI organic growth decelerates below mid-single-digit on *AI substitution* (not FX) AND Ratings issuance craters simultaneously. (NOT "the guidance cut" — that was FX.)
- **RELX:** the **Risk** segment (largest, the real moat) decelerates, or Elsevier shows AI-search disintermediation in the numbers.
- **MCO:** Analytics (smaller, contestable) erodes faster than Ratings cyclicality explains.
- **VRSK:** organic growth fails to re-accelerate off 3.9% over the next ~2 quarters → in-house substitution is structural, not a trough.
- **TRI:** CoCounsel seat-count / Westlaw retention declines disclosed → the legal workflow is migrating to AI-native agents (Harvey), leaving Westlaw a wholesale feed with compressed pricing power.

## Strongest counter (self-pressure, folded in)

Even if the data wall holds, value can migrate from "indispensable workflow" to "one API feed among
many" → **pricing-power compression without revenue loss.** Most acute TRI / RELX-legal; least
SPGI / MCO / CME. So the thesis is **not** "AI can't touch them" — it's "buy where revenue is
CORE-weighted (regulatory / proprietary), be cautious where it's WORKFLOW-weighted." The bull's
trump card: every incumbent is **co-opting the attacker** (TRI on Claude SDK; Moody's / S&P / LSEG /
FactSet / Hebbia as **paid** data providers inside OpenAI's equity-investing plugin) → the
proprietary-data layer may *monetize* AI agents rather than be displaced. No name has yet printed
AI-attributed revenue loss; today's bear case is margin / pricing-power risk + valuation, not booked
decline.

## Net

The AI-disruption de-rate is a **Mr.-Market overreaction on the regulatory / proprietary-core names
(SPGI, RELX, MCO)** and a **genuine open question on the workflow-wrapper name (TRI).** Deploy into
the core-weighted de-rates; size TRI smaller / contingent; treat VRSK as a trough-confirmation play;
drop EQIX from the basket.
