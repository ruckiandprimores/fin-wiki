# AI/Tech Instrument Analyses

> **TL;DR**: The AI/Tech sleeve's instrument universe splits into two baskets that sit in
> **analytical tension**: the same generative-AI capex wave is a **tailwind** for the
> picks-and-shovels (physical AI infrastructure buildout) and a **repricing threat** for the
> data-moats (information/analytics incumbents whose workflow layer AI can substitute). Which
> data-moat names retain a durable *core* franchise under that scrutiny — vs which sit at the
> contestable workflow layer — is resolved name-by-name in
> [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|the dedicated thesis]]. Valuation multiples below are
> a dated snapshot, cordoned separately from the mechanism analysis. *Analysis of mechanism and
> risk — not a recommendation.*

> **Provenance**: 2026-06-27 wiki-as-collector build. Prices = reliable-as-approx June 2026
> snapshots; forward P/Es approximate. Roles tagged per
> [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|the execution-vehicle typology]].

## Watchlist — trigger definitions (durable)

The threshold each name is watched against, and the mechanism it's meant to capture — the
definitions are durable even as the readings against them go stale:

| Name | Trigger definition | What it's meant to capture |
|---|---|---|
| GOOGL | ≤25× fwd P/E AND (DOJ remedies <15% of revenue OR AI Overviews search-revenue stable 3 quarters) | Two legs: antitrust-remedy exposure + AI-Overviews cannibalization risk to Search |
| NVDA | 30%+ drawdown AND 3/3 regime signals (depreciation-policy revision, capex guide-down, mark-to-market deceleration) | A genuine demand-cycle turn, not a price move alone |
| MSCI | ~22–24× fwd P/E | Reversion toward its own historical multiple band |
| ARM | ≤30× fwd P/E AND FTC resolution AND datacenter-mix >20% of revenue | Regulatory overhang + revenue-mix maturation |
| COIN | fwd P/S 3–5× AND EPS stability | The fwd P/E metric breaks in loss-making quarters (denominator collapse); P/S is the secondary gauge |

## Basket analysis — picks-and-shovels (AI physical infrastructure)

Names whose revenue is tied to the physical build-out of AI-driven power, cooling, and
electrification demand — direct beneficiaries of hyperscaler capex regardless of which AI
workloads ultimately win.

| Name | Mechanism / role in the buildout |
|---|---|
| [[investment/instruments/vrt|VRT]] Vertiv | Data-center thermal/power management; backlog +80% YoY, guidance raised — highest fundamental conviction in the basket |
| [[investment/instruments/etn|ETN]] Eaton | Electrical equipment / grid infrastructure for data-center power |
| [[investment/instruments/hubb|HUBB]] Hubbell | Electrical/utility infrastructure components |
| ABB | Industrial electrification and automation, data-center exposure |
| SU.PA Schneider | Energy management and industrial automation, data-center power |

## Basket analysis — data-moats (generative-AI disruption exposure)

Information/analytics incumbents whose economics depend on a proprietary-data or
regulatory-franchise core that AI could substitute at the workflow layer. Whether the core
franchise or only the contestable workflow layer is exposed is a per-name analytical question, not
a table label:

| Name | Mechanism / moat | Durability question |
|---|---|---|
| **[[investment/instruments/relx|RELX]]** | Diversified across Risk / STM (scientific/technical/medical) / Exhibitions | Diversification blunts a single-product AI-disruption read that hits narrower peers harder |
| **[[investment/instruments/vrsk|VRSK]]** Verisk | Insurance-data analytics | Cleanest moat on paper (no AI-disruption headline event to date) but momentum sits in a trough — confirmation pending |
| **TRI** Thomson Reuters | Legal/tax/professional information | Anthropic's legal-AI tool drove a record price drop; ~$143B of client revenue reported "under reconsideration" — a live value-trap risk, unresolved |
| MCO Moody's | Credit ratings — regulatory-franchise moat | Ratings franchises are structurally hard to disintermediate |
| CME | Derivatives exchange — network-effect moat | Exchange network effects are largely AI-resistant by construction |
| [[investment/instruments/spgi|SPGI]] (also Quality Compounder) | Ratings + Market Intelligence — regulatory-franchise moat | The falsifier is *Market Intelligence* organic-growth deceleration specifically attributable to AI substitution — distinct from the FX-driven multiple compression already priced in |

**Note on EQIX**: interconnection/data-center REIT — reclassified out of this basket into the REIT
frame ([[investment/instruments/reits-core-etfs|REIT analysis]]); AI is a demand tailwind for EQIX,
not a disruption exposure, so the moat-durability question above doesn't apply to it.

**Thesis resolution** (detail in [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|the thesis]]): the
thesis's read is that SPGI, RELX, and MCO carry the most durable core franchise through the
de-rate; TRI's case is smaller and contingent on the value-trap question resolving; VRSK is
pending trough confirmation.
