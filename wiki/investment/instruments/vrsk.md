# VRSK — Verisk Analytics

> **TL;DR**: The **best *dataset* moat** in the data-moat complex — a 39-billion-record insurance
> statistical agent that is structurally hard to replicate — but also the **only name with an actual
> decelerating print** (organic **3.9% Q1-26**, mgmt-called a "trough"). So the read is **BUY-THE-TROUGH
> ONLY IF re-acceleration confirms** (rank-4 of the [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|data-moat thesis]]):
> best moat, worst momentum. No AI-disruption *headline* (unlike TRI/RELX-legal), but the deceleration
> is the live question. Dated analysis, not advice.

## Identity

- **Ticker / listing**: VRSK (Nasdaq)
- **What it is**: data/analytics for the **insurance** industry — a regulatory-adjacent statistical
  agent built on a contributory-data network (~39B records) covering underwriting, claims, catastrophe
  and risk modeling. The dataset *is* the product.

## Role

- **Vertical**: AI/Tech data-moat.
- **Execution-vehicle role**: **direct name**, per the [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|typology]].

## Thesis (durable)

The moat is **contributory-data scale** — insurers feed Verisk their loss data, and the aggregate is
worth more than any one contributor's slice; that network is the barrier. It is the cleanest "the
proprietary dataset is the product" case in the complex, with **no generative-AI headline** attached.
The catch: it is also the one name where the *numbers* have softened — AI is **lengthening sales
cycles** and large insurers are **building in-house** analytics, and organic growth printed a 3.9%
trough. So the durability question here is empirical, not narrative: is 3.9% a cyclical trough or the
first sign of in-house substitution? Full ranking context: [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|the thesis]].

## Falsifier / invalidation

- **Organic growth fails to re-accelerate off 3.9% over the next ~2 quarters** → in-house substitution
  is structural, not a trough (the thesis fails; the dataset moat is being disintermediated).

## Risks

- **In-house substitution** — large insurers building their own analytics is the structural bear.
- **AI-lengthened sales cycles** — a demand-timing drag that could mask or precede substitution.
- **Momentum / multiple** — buying a decelerating compounder before re-accel is confirmed is the
  timing risk the "only if confirms" caveat guards.
