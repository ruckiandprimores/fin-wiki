# RELX — RELX plc

> **TL;DR**: RELX's moat rests on a base that is **mostly diversified away from the AI-attacked
> segment** — Legal (LexisNexis) is the segment generative-AI legal tools target, but it sits alongside
> Risk (the largest segment, built on proprietary contributory data no AI model can synthesize) and
> STM/Elsevier (an irreplaceable peer-reviewed archive AI *consumes* rather than substitutes). The
> load-bearing analytical point: the Feb-3 2026 AI-legal-tool headline drove a **−14% single-day move
> (steepest since 1988) across the whole company** for a threat concentrated in a minority segment — a
> clean illustration of how a segment-specific headline can reprice a diversified base as if the whole
> thing were exposed. *Analysis of mechanism and risk — not a recommendation.*

## Identity

- **Ticker / listing**: RELX (LSE primary; RELX ADR on NYSE; Amsterdam)
- **What it is**: global analytics + information-services group. Segments: **Risk** (largest —
  proprietary contributory data, fraud/identity/insurance analytics), **STM** (Elsevier — the
  irreplaceable scientific archive + ScienceDirect/Scopus), **Legal** (LexisNexis — the AI-*attacked*
  segment), **Exhibitions**.
- **Note**: RELX price data was flagged **noisy** in the source sweep (~$31–33 vs $34.46) — confirm the
  live quote/line on a clean re-pull.

## Role

- **Vertical**: AI/Tech data-moat.
- **Execution-vehicle role**: **direct name**. Per the

## Thesis (durable)

**Diversification is the moat-protector.** The generative-AI threat is concentrated on the legal
*workflow* layer (LexisNexis vs Anthropic Claude Cowork / Harvey) — but Legal is a minority segment.
The majority sits in **Risk** (proprietary contributory data — a network of contributed records no AI
can synthesize) and **Elsevier** (an irreplaceable peer-reviewed archive that AI is a *consumer* of,
not a substitute for). So a single legal-product launch repricing the *whole* RELX is the textbook
[[foundation/market-wisdom/mr-market|Mr. Market]] overreaction — the de-rate hit the diversified base
for a minority-segment headline. Full attack/defense reasoning:
[[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|the thesis]].

## Falsifier / invalidation

- **The Risk segment (largest, the real moat) decelerates**, or **Elsevier shows AI-search
  disintermediation in the numbers** (AI answering research queries without the archive).
- LexisNexis seat-count / retention declines disclosed at a magnitude that implies the legal erosion
  is spreading beyond workflow into the contributed-data core.

## Risks

- **Pricing-power compression** in Legal — value migrating from indispensable workflow to a wholesale
  feed even if revenue holds (the workflow-wrapper risk, here confined to a minority segment).
- **FX** — multi-listing / GBP-USD translation noise (and the data-quality flag above).
