# O — Realty Income

> **TL;DR**: A monthly-dividend **triple-net-lease REIT** — the wiki's cleanest worked example of a
> **bond-proxy income compounder**. The load-bearing analytical point: O is **net-lease retail /
> industrial / gaming, not office**, so the **office-CMBS systemic tail that markets lump onto all
> REITs is NOT O's risk** — its real risks are **rates and tenant credit**. Its selloffs are typically
> **rate-driven de-rates** (the lease book stays intact) rather than operating impairments — which is why
> yield-vs-own-history and fwd P/AFFO are the multiples to read here. *Analysis of mechanism and risk —
> not a recommendation.*

## Identity

- **Ticker / listing**: O (NYSE) — "The Monthly Dividend Company."
- **What it is**: a triple-net-lease REIT — ~15,600+ properties on long leases where the **tenant**
  pays taxes, insurance, and maintenance. Tenant base is **retail (non-discretionary / service-heavy),
  industrial, and gaming** — **not office.** S&P 500 + Dividend Aristocrat; monthly dividend.

## Role

- **Vertical**: REITs (direct-first per the [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|typology]] —
  this book frames REITs as direct names, not via a broad REIT ETF).
- **What kind of instrument it is**: a yield vehicle — an income compounder whose price behaves like a
  long-duration bond proxy. That framing is what determines which risks and which multiples matter.

## Thesis (durable)

A **bond-proxy income compounder**: long net leases with contractual escalators + a triple-net
structure that pushes cost inflation to tenants = durable, predictable AFFO and a monthly-growing
dividend. Scale is the moat — cost-of-capital advantage + diversification across thousands of
tenants/properties makes accretive acquisition the growth engine. The analytical tell to watch for is
a **rate-driven de-rate** vs an operating impairment: as a yield vehicle, O re-rates with the long end,
so a wide yield near a multi-year-low price *while the underlying lease book stays intact* is a
de-rate — a fundamentally different signal from a tenant-credit or coverage impairment, even though
both show up as a falling price.

the falsifier below. *Data-confidence: reliable-as-approx June 2026 snapshot.*

## Falsifier / invalidation

- **AFFO per share declines / dividend coverage (AFFO payout) breaks** — the income thesis depends on
  covered, growing AFFO; a coverage break is the core falsifier.
- **Occupancy deteriorates materially** or tenant-credit losses spike (retail bankruptcies cascading
  through the rent roll).
- A structurally **higher-for-longer rate regime** that permanently compresses the spread between O's
  cost of capital and acquisition cap rates (kills the accretive-growth engine, not just the multiple).

## Risks (name-specific — distinct from the REIT-leg macro tail)

- **Interest-rate sensitivity** — the dominant driver of the price; a yield vehicle re-rates with the
  long end.
- **Tenant credit / retail secular pressure** — net-lease retail concentration means tenant
  bankruptcies are the operating risk.
- **NOT office-CMBS** — the 12.34% office delinquency / CRE maturity-wall tail flagged on the
  [[investment/instruments/reits-core-etfs|REIT collector page]] is an office-sector risk; O's
  net-lease retail/industrial book does not carry it directly. (This is the precision a standalone
  page buys over the collector table.)
