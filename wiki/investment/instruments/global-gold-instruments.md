# Ex-US / Global ETF + Gold ETC Analyses

> **TL;DR**: The two diversification / hard-money legs the sweep surfaced as construction gaps
> (the book is 57–71% US via its broad-market wrappers, no gold sleeve). Equity wrappers are
> [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|role-(a)]]; gold ETC is **role-(b)**
> direct-asset-access. The construction finding: a broad "all-world" or "core World" UCITS
> **re-imports** 57–71% US weight, defeating a diversification purpose — a genuine ex-US build
> requires the **explicit ex-US / EM pair** (XUSE + EMIM), not a global blend. Framed as
> concentration-reduction + de-dollarization hedge, **not** an "ex-US wins" call — full case +
> steelman bear in [[investment/theses/ex-us-de-dollarization|the thesis]]. *Analysis, not advice.*

## Global / ex-US equity ETFs (UCITS) — construction analysis

| ETF | Index | US wt | TER | AUM | Construction note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| VWCE/VWRL Vanguard FTSE All-World | All-World DM+EM | 57.0% | 0.22%* | €42.6B | One-fund global core — but re-imports majority US weight |
| IWDA/SWDA iShares Core MSCI World | World (DM) | ~71%† | 0.20% | ~€85B | DM core — even higher US re-import than the all-world blend |
| SPYY SPDR MSCI ACWI | ACWI DM+EM | 60.2% | **0.12%** | €13.8B | Cheapest all-country one-fund; same re-import issue |
| XUSE iShares MSCI World ex-USA | DM ex-US | **0%** | 0.15% | €3.0B | Explicit ex-US developed leg — the construction that actually diversifies |
| EMIM/EIMI iShares Core MSCI EM IMI | EM | **0%** | 0.18% | €29.9B | EM leg, pairs with XUSE |
| ISF iShares Core FTSE 100 | UK | 0% | **0.07%** | €18.0B | Single-country UK — an overlay, not a diversifier |
| ERNA iShares $ Ultrashort Bond | USD IG <1yr | n/a | 0.09% | (large) | Short-duration cash-park |

\*verify 0.19% vs 0.22%; †IWDA US-wt is the standard index figure, confirm live.

**Construction note**: no single "ex-US all-world" UCITS exists at scale — a genuine ex-US
construction requires combining **XUSE + EMIM**, weighted to taste; any single "all-world" or
"core World" fund re-imports the majority-US weight the diversification is meant to reduce.

## Snapshot (as of 2026-06-27 — perishable, illustration only)

> A dated valuation-spread reading — the mechanism (regional discount as a diversification
> argument) does not go stale; these multiples do.

| Region | Fwd P/E | CAPE |
|---|---|---|
| US | ~21–22× | 34.7 |
| Europe / EAFE | ~15–17× | DE 24 / UK 20 |
| EM | ~13× | China 18 / India 34 |

EM-vs-DM discount ~35% reads as roughly the widest in ~15 years; the US-vs-ex-US gap is wide but
off-extreme (SSGA: near its 25-year average). The steelman bear case — the US premium is partly
earned; "cheap ex-US" has been a 10-year value trap; FX cuts both ways for a EUR investor; EM
dispersion is real — is carried in full, not buried, in
[[investment/theses/ex-us-de-dollarization#steelman-bear-case-carried-not-buried|the thesis]]. The
construction case here is about **reducing concentration**, not calling regional outperformance.
