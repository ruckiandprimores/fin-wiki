# SPGI — S&P Global

> **TL;DR**: The **TOP BUY** of the [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|data-moat thesis]] —
> a **regulatory/IP franchise at a de-rated price.** Ratings (NRSRO duopoly with Moody's) + S&P 500
> Indices IP ≈ **50% AI-immune**; the AI-disruption de-rate (28–30× → ~20×) is mostly mispriced
> Mr.-Market fear. The one real soft spot — Market Intelligence (~33%, contestable, slowest-growth) —
> is a deceleration *watch*, not a disclosed loss; the headline "guidance cut" was **FX, not AI.**
> Dated analysis exposing the read, not advice; verify on a clean re-pull before acting.

## Identity

- **Ticker / listing**: SPGI (NYSE)
- **What it is**: ratings, indices, and market-intelligence franchise. Segments: **Ratings** (NRSRO
  credit ratings, ~35% rev, ~AI-immune regulatory franchise), **Indices** (S&P 500 / Dow IP licensing,
  ~14%, ~AI-immune IP), **Market Intelligence** (~33%, data/analytics terminals — the contestable
  layer), Commodity Insights + Mobility (the remainder).
- **Dual-home**: classified both as an **AI/Tech data-moat** ([[investment/instruments/ai-tech-instruments|AI/Tech dossiers]])
  and a **Quality Compounder / Financial** ([[investment/instruments/healthcare-qc-financials|Healthcare·QC·Financials dossiers]]).

## Role

- **Vertical**: AI/Tech data-moat ∩ Quality-Compounder / Financials.
- **Execution-vehicle role**: **direct name** (not a wrapper). Per the

## Thesis (durable)

The moat is **regulatory + IP**, not workflow. NRSRO is a licensed duopoly (with [[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|Moody's]]);
the S&P 500 trademark/IP is a toll the whole index-fund complex pays. **AI is a customer of these,
not a competitor.** ~50% of revenue is structurally AI-immune (Ratings 35% + Indices 14%). The
contestable third (Market Intelligence) is where analyst AI-anxiety attaches — but it is the
*slowest-growth* segment (+8% vs Ratings +13% / Indices +17%), and no AI-driven revenue loss has been
disclosed. The bull's trump card across the complex: incumbents are **co-opting the attacker** (S&P a
*paid* data provider inside OpenAI's equity-investing plugin) → the proprietary-data layer may
monetize AI agents rather than be displaced. Full per-name reasoning:
[[investment/theses/data-moat-ai-disruption|the thesis]].

## Falsifier / invalidation

- **Market Intelligence organic growth decelerates below mid-single-digit on *AI substitution*
  (not FX) AND Ratings issuance craters simultaneously.** (Explicitly **NOT** "the guidance cut" —
  that was an FX trim of reported revenue; SPGI maintained 6–8% organic cc guidance.)
- Loss of NRSRO regulatory status or a credible index-IP challenge (low-probability structural breaks).

## Risks

- **Ratings cyclicality** — issuance volumes track credit markets; a frozen primary market dents the
  AI-immune core for cyclical, not structural, reasons.
- **Pricing-power compression** — even if the data wall holds, value can migrate from indispensable
  workflow toward "one feed among many." Least acute for SPGI (core-weighted) but non-zero on MI.
