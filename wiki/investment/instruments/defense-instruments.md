**Reading the dispersion**: Europe carries more structural runway (a European-preference
procurement policy trend), but that runway is largely already priced into RHM / Saab / Thales; the valuation gap
sits in BAE + Leonardo on the EU side and LMT / GD / NOC on the US side. The open analytical
question — carried, not resolved, in [[investment/theses/defense-rearmament|the thesis]] — is
whether structural re-rating or mean-reversion wins: if the duration-bull case is right, a 35×
multiple on RHM could still prove cheap in hindsight. That unresolved question is the argument for
an ETF-first access vehicle (which diversifies across which side is correct) over concentrating in
the highest-multiple names.
