# REIT + Core/Broad/Bond ETF Analyses

> **TL;DR**: [[investment/instruments/o-realty-income|O (Realty Income)]] is the clearest worked
> example in this set of a **bond-proxy income compounder** — see its dedicated page for the full
> mechanism/risk breakdown. The core-ETF table documents the
> [[foundation/methodology/execution-vehicle-typology|role-(a)]] ballast options + the **SPY
> fee-redundancy** construction finding (the S&P-500 trio is one exposure priced at three
> different fees). The **office-CMBS systemic tail** is a macro overhang that sits on the REIT
> sector broadly but does **not** apply uniformly — O's net-lease book is structurally distinct
> from office exposure (see O's page for that precision). *Dated analysis, not advice.*

**Office / CMBS systemic tail (macro overhang, not O/PSA/WELL-specific)**: office delinquency
12.34%+ (record); 2026 maturity wall $146B (over half may face refinancing stress); the
extend-and-pretend workout mechanism is ending, pushing more loans toward a realized-loss path;
systemic-contagion risk estimated ~15–20%. This tail sits on **office** exposure specifically — it
is the reason a broad REIT-sector wrapper carries risk that a net-lease-only name like O does not
carry directly (see O's page for that distinction).

## Core / broad / bond ETFs (role-a ballast)

| ETF | Tracks | TER | Role | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VOO | S&P 500 | 0.03% | (a) | Core US default |
| IVV | S&P 500 | 0.03% | (a) | = VOO (iShares) |
| SPY | S&P 500 | 0.0945% | (a) | Same 500 stocks as VOO/IVV at ~3× the fee; UIT structure, no internal dividend reinvestment |
| VTI | Total US market | 0.03% | (a) | Core incl mid/small |
| QQQ | Nasdaq-100 | 0.20% | (a)/(c) | Tech-tilted |
| VEA | Developed ex-US | 0.03% | (a) | Ex-US leg |
| IEFA | Developed ex-US (EAFE) | 0.07% | (a) | Near-substitute for VEA |
| VTV | US large value | 0.04% | (c) | Factor tilt |
| VUG | US large growth | 0.04% | (c) | Factor tilt |
| AGG | US aggregate bond | 0.03% | (a) | Bond ballast — short-duration alternatives fit a hawkish-rate regime better than an aggregate-duration fund |

**Construction note**: the S&P-500 trio (VOO/IVV/SPY) is pure fee redundancy — the same 500 stocks
at three fee points, so holding more than one adds cost with no diversification benefit. VEA /
IEFA are near-substitutes for the ex-US leg (see
[[investment/instruments/global-gold-instruments|the dedicated ex-US/EM build]]).
