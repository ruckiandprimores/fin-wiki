# Tenbagger

A stock that returns 10× its purchase price. Coined (or popularized) by Peter Lynch from the baseball term "bagger" (a single = a bag, a four-bagger = a home run).

## Lynch's Usage

> "In small portfolios, even one such tenbagger can produce extraordinary results... A tenbagger of 10 percent of the portfolio adds 100 percentage points to your overall return. With a small portfolio, one or two of these can make a career."

The math: a 10× position at 10% of the portfolio adds 100% to the total return *before* counting the rest of the holdings. This is why fast growers — Lynch's tenbagger source category — are weighted so heavily in his framework.

## Where Tenbaggers Come From

Per Lynch's framework, the highest tenbagger probability comes from:
- **Fast growers** that successfully execute multi-phase expansion (Wal-Mart, Marriott, Limited, Toys "R" Us)
- **Turnarounds** that recover and re-rate (Chrysler 1982 was a 32-bagger from $1.50; Lockheed was a 60-bagger)
- **Asset plays** where hidden value is unlocked by raider/activist/restructuring (Pebble Beach, Newhall Land, Penn Central)
- **Stalwarts at deep recession lows** (Bristol-Myers in 1973–74 was a multibagger from there)

Almost never from:
- Slow growers (math doesn't work — 3% growth doesn't compound to 10×)
- Stalwarts at normal prices (10–12% growth × 5–10 years × multiple compression at the top → maybe 4–5× best case)

## The Generalization

| Returns | Lynch Term |
|---------|-----------|
| 2× | Two-bagger |
| 5× | Five-bagger |
| 10× | Tenbagger |
| 40× | Forty-bagger (Subaru, Dunkin' Donuts) |
| 100× | Hundred-bagger (Waste Management) |
| 200× | Two-hundred-bagger (Amgen, Telecommunications Inc.) |

In the millennium edition's table of 1990s winners, **Dell is an 890-bagger** and **Clear Channel is a 810-bagger** ($10K → $8.9M and $8.1M respectively over 1989–1999). *(See [[foundation/sources/one-up-on-wall-street]] for full table.)*

## In Crypto

Crypto's 10×-plus regime returns happen on much shorter time horizons — Lynch's 7-year tenbagger compresses to 3–18 months in major bull cycles. But the median 10× return time is also accompanied by 90% drawdowns afterward, making the "hold for the tenbagger" rule riskier than in equities. Position-sizing and exit rules matter more.
