# Dollar Milkshake Thesis

Macro framing originating with **Brent Johnson (Santiago Capital)** and adopted by Arthur Hayes as a recurring overlay across his Substack: the US dollar acts as a **liquidity straw** that draws capital out of weaker fiat systems during periods of US-relative monetary tightness, creating dollar strength + emerging-market funding stress. The reverse — US monetary easing — releases the dollar's grip and pushes capital into risk assets including crypto.

## Operational use in the wiki

Hayes's adaptation of the thesis frames **dollar liquidity as the primary BTC driver**:

- **Tightening dollar liquidity (QT, fiscal restraint, capital repatriation)** → BTC down
- **Loosening dollar liquidity (QE, fiscal expansion, SRF/repo, IORB elimination, stablecoin issuance funding T-bill demand)** → BTC up

Per [[foundation/sources/hayes-essays|Hayes essays]] §Theme 1: 5 of 9 curated essays converge on this overlay (Hallelujah, BBC, Frowny Cloud, Fatty Fatty Boom Boom, Long Live the King!).

## Operational signals (composite)

- **MOVE Index >130** = bond-vol regime indicating money-printing trigger likely (per The BBC, Mar 2025)
- **SRF (Standing Repo Facility) usage growth** = stealth-QE conduit (per Hallelujah, Nov 2025)
- **RRP depletion + Treasury Bill issuance acceleration** = fiscal-dominance liquidity injection (~$2.5T drained from RRP into markets via short-bill issuance; per Long Live the King!)
- **Asymmetric QT** (Fed reducing balance sheet but selectively buying 10y while letting short maturities run off) = covert long-yield suppression (per Fatty Fatty Boom Boom)

## Honest caveat

The thesis has been broadly correct since 2014 (BTC tracks dollar liquidity well in retrospect) but has **regime-specific exceptions** — e.g., Hayes himself notes Frowny Cloud (Jan 2026) that Nasdaq decoupled from BTC in 2025 because Trump's industrial-policy AI capex backstop drove Nasdaq independent of dollar-liquidity. Single-axis macro framings always have such exceptions; the thesis is a regime-conditioning input, not a deterministic predictor.

## Lineage

Brent Johnson (Santiago Capital, ~2018 origin) → adopted/popularized by Hayes Substack (2022-present) → wiki ingest 2026-05-08.

Hayes does **not** originate the framing; he uses and crypto-adapts it. Citation discipline: when invoking the thesis in wiki content, attribute origin to Johnson and crypto-application to Hayes.
