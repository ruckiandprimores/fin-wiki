# Circle of Competence

The set of investments an investor can correctly evaluate. Buffett's principle: invest only inside the circle. Knowing the *boundary* of the circle is more important than the circle's *size*.

> "What an investor needs is the ability to correctly evaluate selected businesses... You only have to be able to evaluate companies within your circle of competence. The size of that circle is not very important; knowing its boundaries, however, is vital."
> — Buffett

## Quick test

For any candidate investment, the position is *outside* the circle if any of these is true:
- You can't articulate the business's competitive moat
- You can't articulate what would prove the thesis wrong
- You're acting on borrowed conviction
- You're pattern-matching to a past winner

## Distinguished from related terms

- **Amateur edge** ([[foundation/market-wisdom/amateur-edge|Lynch]]): observation-based input that *generates candidates*; circle of competence is the *filter* on candidates
- **Margin of safety** ([[foundation/market-wisdom/margin-of-safety|Graham]]): pricing buffer in case the circle's analysis is wrong; not a substitute for being inside the circle
- **Locus of control** (psychology): about agency, not analytical capability
