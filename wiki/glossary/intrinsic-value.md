# Intrinsic Value

> "Intrinsic value... can be defined simply: It is the discounted value of the cash that can be taken out of a business during its remaining life."
> — Buffett, 1996 letter

The amount an owner could extract in cash from the business across its remaining lifespan, discounted to present value at an appropriate rate.

## Components

- **Cash extractable** = [[foundation/market-wisdom/owner-earnings|owner earnings]], cumulative, after taxes
- **Remaining life** = how long the business retains its [[foundation/market-wisdom/economic-goodwill|economic goodwill]] / moat
- **Discount rate** = required real return on capital + risk premium

## Three measures, three uses

| Measure | Where it comes from | What it tells you |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Intrinsic value** | DCF of owner earnings | What the business is worth |
| **Book value** | Balance sheet | What was paid in (mostly historical cost) |
| **Market price** | Trading | What buyers are willing to pay today |

The three are rarely equal. Where market price < intrinsic value → opportunity. Where market price > intrinsic value → caution. Book value is mostly diagnostic of capital structure, not value.

## Operational difficulty

Buffett is honest:

> "Calculating intrinsic value is neither easy nor objective. It depends on estimation of both future cash flows and interest rate movements."

Two analysts with the same facts will produce different intrinsic-value estimates. Both can be defensible. The goal is *vaguely right* (Keynes), not *precisely wrong*.

## Crypto translation

For a token, intrinsic value would require:
- Discounted future protocol revenue net of [[foundation/market-wisdom/owner-earnings|necessary emissions]]
- Estimate of moat durability (governance ossification, fork resistance, network effect persistence)
- Discount rate accounting for crypto's tail risks (regulatory, cryptographic, governance attack)

Most crypto valuations skip all three. "TVL ratio" or "P/Fees" comparisons don't compute intrinsic value — they compute multiples relative to peer protocols, which is just relative valuation.
