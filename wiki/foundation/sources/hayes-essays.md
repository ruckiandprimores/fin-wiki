# Hayes Essays — Arthur Hayes / Maelstrom Substack archive

> **TL;DR**: The wiki's first **crypto-native primary source**. Arthur Hayes (BitMEX co-founder; co-designer of the perp swap; Maelstrom CIO) writes a recurring Substack from 2022–present that blends macro-overlay mechanism content (dollar liquidity → BTC, fiscal dominance, stablecoin → treasury flow, AI-displacement → bank credit cascade) with funding/basis-mechanic primary-source material (perp design history, funding-as-basis-tracker, Hyperliquid valuation framework). **9 essays curated** from the visible Substack archive (2024–2026 window). **Reduced scope vs original spec**: foundational classics from BitMEX-blog era ("Dollar Milkshake," "Maelstrom" intro, March 2020 / 2021 / 2022 / 2023 cascade post-mortems) are not accessible — the BitMEX blog has been migrated and the Substack archive only pages back to 2024-2025. The wiki gets contemporary regime calibration + perp-mechanic primary-source from "Adapt or Die" (the Substack's foundational-shape essay), not the historical cascade narratives the spec hoped for.

## Why This Source Matters For The Wiki

Three structural gaps closed:

1. **Zero-coverage crypto-native gap**. The wiki had 11 ingested sources, all equity/macro/microstructure-derived. Worldview Prior #1 (crypto ≈ pre-1980s US equities) covered the *historical-analog* dimension via Lefèvre/Wyckoff/Kindleberger but had no *contemporary regime calibration source*. Every wiki claim about "current crypto behavior" was AI-summarization-of-secondary-sources, not practitioner-primary. Hayes closes this — his BitMEX co-design experience makes him the perp-mechanic primary source; his current trading desk makes him the contemporary calibration source.

2. **Macro-overlay layer for trading**. The wiki's macro pages (`foundation/macro/`) had been built from Kindleberger / Buffett / Graham — equity-cycle macro. Hayes adds **dollar-liquidity → crypto-asset transmission** as the operational macro overlay. Five of the 9 curated essays converge on this theme (Hallelujah, BBC, Frowny Cloud, Fatty Fatty Boom Boom, Long Live the King!). The framework is now wiki-encoded with multiple-essay redundancy.

3. **Cycle-calibration with non-USD lever**. Hayes's "Long Live the King!" essay introduces the under-appreciated point that the 2015-17 BTC cycle was driven by **yuan credit expansion**, not Fed policy. This decouples the four-year halving cycle from any single monetary axis and reframes BTC as **whichever-currency-is-expanding's-fastest-monetary-vent**. This is genuinely additive vs the wiki's existing cycle framework.

The book's framing principle (Hayes's recurring voice across 9 essays):

> "Bitcoin trades solely based on the market expectation for the future supply of fiat." (The BBC, Mar 31 2025)

That principle maps onto the wiki's Part I §4 Mechanism-First framing — but with *fiat-supply expectations* as the load-bearing variable for BTC specifically, distinguishing from equity-cycle drivers.

## Mechanism Themes Extracted

Organized by theme rather than essay, since multiple essays converge on each theme:

### Theme 1 — Dollar liquidity → BTC transmission (5 essays)

The wiki's **strongest crypto-applicable macro overlay**, with multi-essay redundancy.

**Mechanism**: BTC tracks expectations of future fiat (USD primary, yuan secondary) supply. Dollar liquidity expansion via any channel — Fed QE, SRF/repo, fiscal deficit monetization, IORB elimination, stablecoin issuance funding T-bill demand — drives BTC up. Contraction (QT, fiscal restraint, capital controls forcing dollar repatriation) drives BTC down.

**Operational signals (composite from essays)**:
- **MOVE Index >130** = money-printing trigger threshold (per The BBC, with caveat that this is observational not formally validated)
- **SRF (Standing Repo Facility) usage** = stealth-QE conduit (per Hallelujah; cumulative usage proxies the channel without showing on Fed balance sheet)
- **RRP depletion + Treasury Bill issuance acceleration** = fiscal-dominance liquidity injection (per Long Live the King!: ~$2.5T drained from RRP into markets via short-bill issuance)
- **Asymmetric QT** (Fed reducing balance sheet but selectively buying 10y while letting short maturities run off) = covert long-yield suppression (per Fatty Fatty Boom Boom)
- **Asian carry-trade unwind** (TWD/KRW strengthening; capital repatriation) = forced dollar selling (per Fatty Fatty Boom Boom)

Cross-link target: [[foundation/macro/btc-regime-taxonomy-2020-2025]] — these signals belong as regime markers; ingest as live-regime entry markers when next updating that page.

### Theme 2 — Perp funding mechanics (1 essay, primary source)

**Adapt or Die** is the primary-source narrative on perp design. Key mechanism claims (Hayes co-built this):

- **Funding rate as basis-tracker**: "If the perp traded at an average 1% premium to spot over the last eight hours … longs would pay 1% to shorts." (Adapt or Die) — explains how basis-tracking funding prevents persistent perp-spot mispricing
- **Lookback-index for synthetic-dollar shortage**: 2016 when BTC's rapid appreciation created shortage of synthetic dollars, BitMEX implemented "a look back index that recorded the basis between the swap and spot" (Adapt or Die) — historical operational fact
- **Leverage-vs-collateral tradeoff**: high-leverage perps mean clients keep less collateral on exchange, reducing security-fund-required-capital vs TradFi clearing model (Adapt or Die)

Cross-link target: [[trading/mechanisms/funding-cycle-dynamics]] — Hayes's primary-source perspective adds origin-story authority to the funding-cycle-dynamics page; cite Adapt or Die in the §Mechanism subsection.

### Theme 3 — Stablecoin → treasury flow (1 essay)

**Quid Pro Stablecoin** maps the bank-issued-stablecoin → T-bill-demand pathway:

- **Deposit conversion flow**: regular bank deposits convert to JPMD-class bank-issued stablecoins (under Treasury Secretary Bessent's stablecoin policy framework), freeing up "$6.8 trillion" in treasury-buying capacity
- **IORB elimination**: removing Fed interest-on-reserves "would force banks to replace that lost interest income by converting reserves into treasuries," releasing "$3.3 trillion" for T-bill purchases

**Implication for crypto**: as stablecoins (USDT/USDC plus future bank-issued variants) become the primary T-bill buyer, **stablecoin issuance is structurally bid by the US fiscal funding need**. This implies persistent stablecoin supply expansion regardless of crypto-internal demand — i.e., a permanent dollar-liquidity injection conduit into the crypto ecosystem.

Cross-link target: macro pages + [[glossary/basis-trade-crypto]].

### Theme 4 — Cycle calibration with non-USD lever (1 essay)

**Long Live the King!** decouples the BTC cycle from a single-currency framework:

- "Bitcoin skyrocketed because of a fuck ton of yuan sloshing around the global money markets" (2015-17)
- "The supply of dollars rose sharply and peaked and rolled over at the end of 2013" (Genesis cycle)
- "The future as painted by the ruling political elite points to lower, not higher, interest rates and higher, not lower, growth in the money supply." (current cycle thesis)

**Mechanism**: BTC responds to whichever monetary system is expanding most aggressively at the time. Four-year halving-cycle framing is regime-conditional — it appears to "fail" in the current cycle because *both* dollar and yuan systems are easing simultaneously, so no clear bear-phase trigger.

Cross-link target: [[foundation/market-wisdom/bubble-cycle-stages]] §Stage 2 (Boom/Credit Expansion) needs the multi-currency-credit-impulse refinement.

### Theme 5 — AI-displacement → cascade-trigger speculation (1 essay)

**This Is Fine** offers a forward-looking cascade-trigger thesis:

- AI-displacement of ~20% of 72.1M US knowledge workers (~14.4M jobs) → consumer-credit and mortgage default cascade
- $330B consumer credit losses + $227B mortgage losses = $557B total = ~13% write-down vs total US commercial bank equity
- Triggers regional bank failures + credit contraction → forces Fed emergency expansion → eventual BTC upside

**This is speculative**, not historical. But it's mechanistic — Hayes specifies the magnitude, cohort, and feedback path. As a forward-looking cascade-trigger candidate it belongs as a regime-watch input, not as established history.

Cross-link target: [[foundation/market-structure/bubbles-crashes-circuit-breakers]] §cascade-trigger taxonomy as a forward-watch addition.

### Theme 6 — Perp DEX vs CEX valuation (1 essay)

**$HYPE Man** offers a quantitative framework for evaluating perp-DEX equity-equivalents:

- **Revenue distribution model**: 97% of Hyperliquid fees → $HYPE buybacks; structural support via earnings (vs purely-speculative tokens)
- **ADV/OI ratio as wash-volume detector**: low ratio (low daily-volume relative to capital-locked open interest) = organic activity; high ratio = wash-trading-likely
- **HIP-3 permissionless perp listings**: equities, commodities, metals tradable 24/7 on-chain (off-hours arbitrage opportunity vs TradFi closure)
- **Valuation comparable**: P/E 12x for Hyperliquid vs CME/Coinbase 26-40x → re-rating room if revenue persists

Cross-link target: [[foundation/market-structure/dealer-economics]] — perp-DEX dealer economics is a sibling to CEX market-making; ADV/OI as a venue-quality indicator deserves a glossary mention.

## Honest Caveats

- **Reduced scope vs original task spec**. The spec mentioned foundational essays from BitMEX-blog era ("Dollar Milkshake," "Maelstrom" intro, March 2020 / 2021 / 2022 / 2023 cascade post-mortems). These returned 404 on the Substack and the BitMEX blog has been migrated such that direct archive access is broken. Wayback Machine fallback was not attempted (per user direction). Wiki gets contemporary-calibration but not the historical cascade narratives.
- **Archive selection bias**. The Substack visible archive only goes back to ~late 2024. Earlier Substack essays (2022–2024) are not visible via the public archive view (pagination broken beyond page 1). Curation is therefore from a 2024–2026 window — heavy on macro-overlay, lighter on historical mechanism reviews. May matter when calibrating against earlier cycles.
- **Punditry-titled wrapping**. Hayes's stylistic signature is to open with metaphor / personal anecdote (skiing, cooking, dating profiles) before pivoting to mechanism analysis. Title-based triage substantially under-counts mechanism content. The 9 curated essays were verified by content-spot-check, not title.
- **Trade thesis non-extraction discipline**. Hayes regularly publishes specific trade ideas (e.g., target prices, position sizing recommendations). Per task spec these are **explicitly not extracted** — they are time-bound and don't compound. Wiki extracts only the underlying mechanism framing, not the trade.
- **Voice not adopted**. Hayes is irreverent, dollar-skeptical, hedge-fund-practitioner. Wiki extraction is operational-mechanism-only; the voice belongs to Hayes, not the wiki.
- **Macro-thesis is forward-looking and unverified**. Several mechanism claims (asymmetric QT magnitude, AI-displacement cascade, capital-controls tax revenue) are predictions or hypothetical scenarios, not validated history. Wiki should treat them as **regime-watch inputs** (worth tracking when the predicted condition appears), not as established mechanisms.
- **Confirmation bias in selection**. Hayes is dollar-bearish and BTC-bullish by long-running thesis; his framings naturally support that direction. Pair with counter-perspective sources (e.g., dollar-bull macro practitioners) when the wiki ingests its next macro-axis source. Hayes is not the only voice; current state of the wiki has no counter-perspective.
- **Single-author lineage risk**. Hayes is one practitioner. The wiki's behavioral-pre-regulation cluster has multiple authors confirming each other (Lefèvre + Wyckoff + Kindleberger). Hayes alone on the crypto-native axis is structurally fragile — a future correction in his thesis would propagate widely. Companion crypto-native sources (CoinMetrics, on-chain primer literature) are queued for follow-up.
