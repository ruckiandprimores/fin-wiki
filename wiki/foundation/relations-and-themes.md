# Cross-Page Relations and Recurring Themes

> **TL;DR**: The wiki has 42 content pages across 4 ingested sources, but the *deepest insights* live in the relations between pages, not in any single page. This page maps the recurring themes that span multiple sources, the methodological tensions worth knowing about, and the signal-stacking patterns that combine pages into actionable trading frameworks. Complement to [[foundation/sources/lineage]] (which maps source-level lineage) — this page maps page-level themes.

## What This Page Is

The [[foundation/sources/lineage|lineage page]] documents how *sources* relate (Graham → Buffett, etc.). This page documents how *concepts* relate across pages. Many high-value insights aren't on any one page — they emerge from noticing the same pattern in three or four different places.

Updated when:
- A new ingest produces concepts that converge on existing themes
- New cross-page tensions emerge
- A signal-stacking pattern proves valuable in practice

**Implication**: a strategy without a crowd-psychology mechanism is just a backtest. Per Part I Section 4, every strategy should articulate WHO is moving money, WHY, and WHEN — and the answer is *always* a crowd-psychology mechanism, even when dressed in quantitative clothing.

## Theme 2 — Concentration vs. Diversification (Unresolved Tension)

Different sources give different answers. The tension is real and per-context.

| Source | Position | Page |
|--------|----------|------|
| Graham (defensive) | Diversify — 10–30 stocks meeting all 7 criteria | [[investment/allocation/defensive-investor-criteria]] |
| Graham (defensive portfolio) | 50/50 stocks/bonds; mechanical rebalance | [[investment/allocation/defensive-investor-portfolio]] |
| Graham (enterprising) | Concentrate in bargains, special situations | [[investment/allocation/enterprising-investor]] |
| Lynch | "Number of holdings = number of attractive theses you've found" — 3-10 for amateurs, 1,400 for Magellan | [[investment/allocation/lynch-portfolio-design]] |
| Buffett | "Concentrate, both portfolio and mind" — 5–10 names with deep understanding | [[foundation/market-wisdom/modern-finance-theory-critique]] |
| Buffett (Munger) | Wonderful business at fair price → patient hold; few names | [[investment/allocation/wonderful-business-fair-price]] |
| Murphy | Doesn't take a position; focused on per-trade execution | — |

**Resolution candidates** (none universally accepted):
- *Effort-based*: low effort → diversify (Graham defensive, index funds); high effort → concentrate (Buffett, enterprising Graham)
- *Conviction-based*: low conviction → diversify; high conviction → concentrate (this is Lynch's framing)
- *Phase-based*: bull market → concentrate; bear market → diversify (informal trader rule)

**Operational rule for this wiki's user**: per the Apr 28 the wiki architecture, the trader is targeting day/swing trading where *position sizing per trade* matters more than portfolio breadth. Buffett's "concentration of mind" applies: deeply understand the few mechanisms you trade rather than spread thin across many.

## Theme 3 — Multi-Signal Confirmation Discipline

Not articulated as a standalone concept anywhere in the wiki, but operationally appears in every source.

| Source | Confirmation pattern | Page |
|--------|----------------------|------|
| Lynch | 13 attributes (no stock has all; 4-5 = candidate) | [[foundation/market-wisdom/perfect-stock-attributes]] |
| Lynch | Per-category sell rules (multiple signals, not one) | [[investment/allocation/when-to-sell-by-category]] |
| Buffett | Circle of competence test (7 questions, all must pass) | [[foundation/market-wisdom/circle-of-competence]] |
| Murphy | Dow tenet 4: averages must confirm | [[foundation/market-wisdom/dow-theory-and-ta-premises]] |
| Murphy | Pattern + volume + oscillator stack | [[trading/mechanisms/chart-patterns]] |
| Murphy | 4-layer crypto cross-market stack | [[foundation/macro/intermarket-analysis]] |
| Murphy | Three-source crypto volume (spot + perp + on-chain) | [[trading/mechanisms/volume-analysis]] |

**The principle**: any single signal is low-conviction; signals stacked across mechanisms / time-scales / data sources are high-conviction. This emerges as a *meta-discipline* across all four sources.

**Practical rule**: never enter a trade or hold a position on a single signal. The minimum stack: one trend filter + one entry signal + one confirmation signal. Higher conviction = more orthogonal signals agreeing.

This converges with the wiki architecture's emphasis on **mechanism-first framing** (Part I Section 4): a stacked signal forces you to articulate the mechanism each layer captures.

## Theme 4 — Time Horizon Determines What's True

Not always made explicit, but every Murphy/Buffett apparent contradiction dissolves when time horizon is added.

| Time horizon | Dominant framework | What's true |
|--------------|--------------------|-----------|
| Intraday — week | Murphy (TA), Lynch's cyclical timing | Trends persist; pattern recognition; momentum |
| Week — month | Murphy + Lynch + intermarket analysis | Regime detection; mean reversion at extremes |
| Month — year | Lynch six categories + Murphy regime + Buffett | Earnings drive price; category rules apply |
| Year — decade | Buffett wonderful-business + Graham margin of safety | Fundamentals dominate; quality compounds |
| Decade+ | Graham theoretical foundation + Buffett's owner economics | Business performance ≈ stock performance |

**Why apparent contradictions dissolve:**
- Murphy "market discounts everything" (true short-term) vs. Buffett "market is frequently inefficient" (true long-term) — both correct at their horizons
- Murphy "trends persist" (true short-term) vs. Graham "no one consistently follows market" (true long-term) — different claim, different horizon
- Lynch "water the weeds" (compound through trend persistence) vs. Graham "rebalance mechanically" (mean revert at asset-class level) — different abstraction levels

**Operational rule**: identify which horizon you're operating at *before* picking your framework. The trader's day/swing horizon (≤1 week) makes Murphy + cyclical timing the dominant framework, with Lynch/Buffett providing structural context.

## Theme 5 — Real Earnings vs. Headline Numbers

A discipline that runs through Lynch and Buffett but is less explicit in Graham/Murphy.

| Source | Real-earnings concept | Page |
|--------|------------------------|------|
| Lynch | Per-share-vs-headline; the Navistar warning (operational recovery + dilution = no per-share recovery) | [[foundation/market-wisdom/perfect-stock-attributes]] (#13 buybacks); [[glossary/diworseification]] |
| Buffett | Owner earnings = reported + non-cash − maintenance capex | [[foundation/market-wisdom/owner-earnings]] |
| Buffett | Look-through earnings (parent + investee proportional) | [[foundation/sources/buffett-essays]] |
| Buffett | Economic vs. accounting goodwill | [[foundation/market-wisdom/economic-goodwill]] |
| Graham | Inflation eroding nominal returns | [[foundation/macro/inflation-and-investments]] |

**The pattern**: every "headline" financial metric has a hidden adjustment that reveals the real economics. The discipline of finding the adjustment is what separates investing from speculation.

**Crypto translation**: protocol revenue is the headline; real "owner earnings" subtracts emissions used to maintain the protocol. Most crypto analytics dashboards report only the headline. The wiki's strongest single mechanism for crypto valuation is [[foundation/market-wisdom/owner-earnings|owner-earnings translation to protocol-revenue-minus-emissions]].

## Theme 6 — Avoid the Crowd's Trades

All four sources converge on this, with different specific advice.

| Source | "Avoid" advice | Page |
|--------|----------------|------|
| Graham | Avoid IPOs, hot industries, formula investing | [[investment/allocation/enterprising-investor]] |
| Lynch | Avoid: hot stocks, next-something, diworseifications, whisper stocks, exciting names | [[foundation/market-wisdom/stocks-to-avoid-lynch]] |
| Buffett | Avoid: outside circle, MFT-driven thinking, institutional-imperative-driven decisions | [[foundation/market-wisdom/circle-of-competence]], [[foundation/market-wisdom/institutional-imperative]] |
| Murphy | Avoid: counter-trend trades; don't trade against the macro grain | [[foundation/macro/intermarket-analysis]] |

**The pattern**: when "everyone" is doing X, X has high reputation risk *and* low expected edge.

**Critical caveat**: this isn't a contrarian-by-default rule — sometimes the crowd is right. The discipline is to ask *why* you're taking the trade and check whether the answer is "because everyone is" (bad) or "because the mechanism shows..." (good).

## Theme 8 — The Value-Investing / Technical-Analysis Synthesis

Conventionally framed as opposing schools. The wiki's structure shows them as complementary.

| What value investing answers | What technical analysis answers |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| What is this worth? | When does it move? |
| What's the long-term return? | Where's the entry? |
| Should I own this for 10 years? | Should I trade this now? |
| Earnings, moats, management | Price, volume, momentum |
| Buffett, Lynch, Graham | Murphy, ✅ Wyckoff (ingested 2026-05-03), ✅ Livermore (ingested 2026-05-03), ✅ Kindleberger (ingested 2026-05-04 — bubble dynamics dimension) |

**Applied to the trader's task**: per the wiki architecture (Apr 28 update), the trader has a working idea→DSL→backtest pipeline; the bottleneck is *idea quality* not implementation. The synthesis the wiki argues for:

1. **Value-investing layer** generates *theses* — which assets have moats / mispriced fundamentals / non-obvious value
2. **Technical-analysis layer** generates *triggers* — when to enter / exit a thesis-aligned trade

Neither alone is sufficient. The trader who only uses TA trades noise. The investor who only uses fundamentals catches falling knives. **The combination is the wiki's overall argument**.

The 4-layer crypto stack ([[foundation/macro/intermarket-analysis]]) is the operational form of this synthesis — macro regime + crypto regime + sector regime + asset signal — combining the fundamentals/regime layers with the TA-execution layer.

## Tensions Worth Tracking

These remain genuinely unresolved. Productive tensions, not bugs.

### Tension 1 — Murphy's Premise 1 vs. Lynch/Buffett's Mechanism-First

- Murphy: "Why prices move doesn't matter; just study price." (premise 1)
- Lynch / Buffett / the wiki architecture (ARCHITECTURE.md): "Every strategy must articulate WHY (the mechanism)."

**Possible resolution**: different stages of the trading pipeline. Lynch/Buffett mechanism-first applies to *strategy generation* (idea quality control). Murphy's price-only applies to *execution* (you don't need to know why for the entry). Compatible if applied at different stages, but worth being explicit about which stage you're in.

### Tension 2 — Concentration vs. Diversification

Already discussed in Theme 2. Genuinely unresolved at the universal level; resolved per-context.

### Tension 3 — "Watering the Weeds" (Lynch) vs. Mean Reversion (Graham implicit)

Already flagged in [[investment/allocation/lynch-portfolio-design|lynch-portfolio-design]] and [[investment/allocation/defensive-investor-portfolio|defensive-investor-portfolio]]. Resolved per-category (stalwarts mean-revert, fast growers compound). The deeper resolution is in [[foundation/market-wisdom/value-vs-growth-false-dichotomy|value-vs-growth-false-dichotomy]]: growth is value-creating only when ROE > cost of capital.

### Tension 4 — Buffett's "Wonderful Business" vs. Lynch's "Cigar-Butt-by-Category"

Lynch is more permissive than Buffett. Lynch's six categories include *all* business types — slow growers, even — at appropriate prices. Buffett would skip Lynch's slow growers and most stalwarts at full prices. Resolution: scale dependence (Buffett can't deploy Berkshire's capital into small fast growers; Lynch could). User's scale aligns with Lynch's permissiveness more than Buffett's selectivity.

## Signal-Stacking Patterns That Emerge

Combinations of pages that compose into high-conviction setups.

### Setup A — Crypto cyclical-timing entry

Stack of:
- [[trading/mechanisms/cyclical-timing]] (cycle position trough — funding negative, sentiment capitulation)
- [[trading/mechanisms/support-resistance]] (price at major support — round number, on-chain URPD cluster, prior low)
- [[trading/mechanisms/oscillators-divergence]] (bullish divergence on 4h or daily)
- [[trading/mechanisms/volume-analysis]] (volume expansion on bounce)
- [[foundation/macro/intermarket-analysis]] (macro layer aligned — DXY weakening, M2 expanding)

Five-layer stack. Rare to all align. When they do, highest-conviction long entry the wiki produces.

### Setup B — Trend-continuation swing entry

Stack of:
- [[trading/mechanisms/moving-averages]] (4h 20/50 EMA bullish bias)
- [[trading/mechanisms/chart-patterns]] (bull flag forming)
- [[trading/mechanisms/oscillators-divergence]] (hidden bullish divergence on pullback)
- [[trading/mechanisms/volume-analysis]] (volume contracting through flag → expanding on breakout)
- [[trading/mechanisms/support-resistance]] (pullback to broken-resistance-now-support)

The mechanical day/swing setup. Applicable across crypto, FX, equities.

### Setup C — Lynch fast-grower investment selection

Stack of:
- [[foundation/market-wisdom/lynch-six-categories]] (categorize as fast grower)
- [[foundation/market-wisdom/perfect-stock-attributes]] (passes 4-5 of 13 attributes)
- [[foundation/market-wisdom/circle-of-competence]] (within investor's competence)
- [[foundation/market-wisdom/economic-goodwill]] (has identifiable moat)
- [[investment/allocation/wonderful-business-fair-price]] (price reasonable relative to growth)

Five-layer stack producing a position with both Lynch's categorical fit and Buffett's quality discipline.

### Setup D — Sell discipline (mechanical exit)

Stack of:
- [[investment/allocation/when-to-sell-by-category]] (category-specific signal triggered)
- [[trading/mechanisms/oscillators-divergence]] (bearish divergence at recent high)
- [[trading/mechanisms/support-resistance]] (price at major resistance)
- [[trading/mechanisms/volume-analysis]] (declining volume on recent rally)
- [[foundation/market-wisdom/institutional-imperative]] (signs of late-cycle institutional accumulation)

Triple confirmation that exit is appropriate, not just price-anchored.

## How to Use This Page

**When designing a trade**: scan the signal-stacking setups (A-D). Aim to reproduce one with current market signals.

**When encountering an apparent contradiction in the wiki**: check Tensions section. Most are resolved at deeper levels.

**When identifying a missing concept**: scan Themes — if the concept fits an existing theme, link it; if not, the wiki may have a structural gap worth filling.

**When evaluating a new ingest**: ask which themes it strengthens, which tensions it resolves, which gaps it fills. Pages that do all three are highest leverage.
