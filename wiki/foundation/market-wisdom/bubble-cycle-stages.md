# Bubble Cycle Stages — Displacement → Boom → Euphoria → Distress → Panic

> **TL;DR**: The canonical 5-stage Minsky-Kindleberger bubble framework. Every speculative cycle in 400 years of recorded history has followed this sequence with structural fidelity even when circumstantial details differ. **Operational use in this wiki**: regime-conditioning — knowing which stage a market is in changes which mechanisms work. Trend-following dominates in Boom; volatility expansion + breakdown setups dominate in Distress; liquidation-cascade aftermath dominates in Panic. From [[foundation/sources/kindleberger-manias-panics-crashes|Kindleberger]] Ch 2, building on [Minsky's Financial Instability Hypothesis](https://www.levyinstitute.org/pubs/wp74.pdf).

## The Five Stages (Quick Reference)

| Stage | One-line description | Strategy implication for day/swing |
|-------|---------------------|------------------------------------|
| **1. Displacement** | Exogenous shock creates real new profit opportunity | Position-watch new sector flows; do not yet trade momentum |
| **2. Boom (credit expansion)** | Credit accommodates the displacement; rational rise | Trend-following works; volatility low; pullbacks bought |
| **3. Euphoria** | "This time is different"; outsiders pile in; speculation for gains | Trend-following still works but with rising tail risk; tighten stops; exit-by-mechanism, not target |
| **4. Distress** | First cracks; insider/outsider flow imbalance peaks | Volatility-expansion plays; breakdown setups; sit-tight on shorts |
| **5. Panic / Revulsion** | Door-shut cascade; only price exhaustion / halts / LOLR end it | Liquidation-cascade-aftermath plays (Wyckoff spring setup); position-size carefully; long-side capitulation entry on multi-channel confluence |

## Why This Framework Matters Operationally

Most trading frameworks assume a single market regime. They work in one regime and fail in others. Pivotal-point breakouts work magnificently in Boom; they generate constant false signals in Distress. Mean-reversion works cleanly in late Distress / early Panic; it crashes during Euphoria as everything trends.

The 5-stage framework is the **regime-conditioning input** that tells you which strategy family to use:

- **Boom** → trend-following, breakout entries, [[trading/mechanisms/pivotal-points|pivotal-point breakouts]]
- **Euphoria** → still trend-following but with [[foundation/market-wisdom/sit-tight-discipline|sit-tight discipline]] tightening; defensive position sizing; alert for distribution signs ([[foundation/market-wisdom/composite-operator|Wyckoff CO]])
- **Distress** → [[trading/mechanisms/group-leader-divergence|group-leader-divergence]] shorts; reaction-on-rally fades; vol-expansion plays
- **Panic** → [[trading/mechanisms/accumulation-distribution|spring/upthrust]] mechanisms become active; liquidation-cascade aftermath setup

For the trader's day/swing horizon, **stage identification is the most important regime input** — more than time-of-day, more than asset selection, more than position size.

## Reconciling the Variants

Ch 2's actual sequence is: **Displacement → Boom (credit-fueled) → Euphoria (with overtrading sub-stage) → Distress → Revulsion/Panic**. Some textbook summaries split out "Credit Expansion" as its own stage to make a five-step mnemonic. The substance is identical. The wiki uses the canonical 5-label sequence below; "credit expansion" is treated as the Boom-stage *mechanism*, not a separate stage.

## Stage 1 — Displacement

**Description.** An *exogenous* shock creates real new profit opportunities in at least one important sector. The shock can be technological (railways, internet, smart contracts), monetary (rate cuts, financial liberalization), regulatory (deregulation, ETF approval), geopolitical (war start/end), or demographic (new entrants to the asset class).

> "Bubbles start with a displacement — some sort of a shock to the system, which could be war, a major political change, deregulation, a technological innovation, a financial innovation or a shift in monetary policy." — Pragmatic Capitalism summary

The displacement is *real*. Bubble formation requires a real seed; bubbles are what the system does *with* a genuine shock, not what fakes do to nothing.

### Telltale market signatures
- Outperformance concentrated in the displaced sector
- New listings, new fund launches, new exchanges or vehicles tied to the displacement
- Story-driven returns ahead of fundamentals
- Authorities not yet rationalizing — the displacement is "interesting" but not yet "the new economy"

### Telltale credit/macro signatures
- Loose monetary stance OR a new credit channel opens
- Sometimes the displacement *is* the credit channel (financial liberalization)

### Historical examples

| Era | Displacement | Sector |
|-----|--------------|--------|
| 1630s Netherlands | Tulip cultivation + East India trade wealth | Tulip bulbs |
| 1720 France | John Law's monetary innovation + Mississippi colonization | Mississippi Co. shares |
| 1720 England | South Sea Company debt-for-equity swap | South Sea shares |
| 1840s UK | Steam-rail technology | Railway equities |
| 1873 Vienna/NYC | Post-Franco-Prussian-War liquidity + railway expansion | Railroads, banks |
| 1907 NYC | Trust-company financial innovation outside Fed-precursor regulation | Trust shares |
| 1920s US | Auto + electrification + telephone + Federal Reserve policy | Equities, FL real estate |
| 1980s Japan | Financial liberalization + Plaza Accord yen revaluation | RE + equities |
| 1980s Nordic | Financial liberalization | RE + equities |
| 1990s Asia | Yen appreciation + financial liberalization in Thailand/Korea | RE + equities + currency |
| 1995–2000 US | Internet commercialization (1993 Mosaic; 1995 Netscape IPO) | Tech equities |
| 2003–2007 US | Mortgage securitization + low Fed rates + China savings recycle | Housing |

### Crypto cycle displacements

| Cycle | Approx peak | Displacement |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 2013 | Apr/Nov 2013 | Cypriot bank-deposit haircuts + Silk Road / Bitcoin awareness + China retail entry |
| 2017 | Dec 2017 | **Ethereum + ERC-20 smart-contract token issuance** = ICO mechanism; >$4B raised in last 8 months of 2017 |
| 2020–2021 | Apr 2021 / Nov 2021 | **Multiple compounding displacements**: COVID monetary expansion + DeFi summer + NFT + Robinhood retail explosion + institutional adoption (Tesla, MicroStrategy, Coinbase IPO) |
| 2024–? | TBD | Bitcoin spot ETF approval Jan 2024 + Ethereum spot ETF + institutional desk participation + Trump pro-crypto stance + AI/blockchain narratives |

### Crypto carrier (current observable)

For each candidate displacement, watch:
- New sector ETF inflows (per BlackRock IBIT, Ethereum spot ETFs)
- Narrative concentration in CT/Twitter sentiment
- New chain TVL launching from low base (Solana 2024; new L2s)
- On-chain new-address growth in a specific cohort
- BTC dominance shifts signaling rotation into a new narrative

### Operational rule

When a displacement is real (e.g., spot ETFs genuinely opened new pools of capital), the early-to-mid Boom stage may be rational. **Bubble-detection should focus on the transition from Boom to Euphoria** — when credit expansion and outsider participation start to extrapolate the displacement linearly into the future. *The displacement being real doesn't prevent the bubble; it is the precondition for one.*

## Stage 2 — Boom (Credit Expansion)

**Description.** Credit accommodates the displacement. Asset prices rise. Investment increases. Income growth accelerates. The loop is *initially rational* — fundamentals genuinely improve. This stage may last quarters to years.

> "While the potential for profit is created by a displacement, it is fueled further by an expansion of bank credit, with more loans being made and the total money supply increasing." — Pragmatic Capitalism

### Telltale market signatures
- Trend-following works
- Volatility low
- Drawdowns shallow and bought
- Lengthening list of sectors participating
- New entrants reporting strong returns

### Telltale credit/macro signatures
- Bank lending growth accelerates above nominal GDP
- New lenders form to capture share
- Margin requirements ease
- Down payments fall
- Loan covenants loosen
- **Spreads compress**

### Historical examples
- 1920s NYSE 1925–1928
- Japan equities 1985–1988
- US housing 2003–2006
- Dot-com 1995–1998

### Crypto carrier (current observable)
- **Open interest on perps grows faster than spot market cap**
- **Funding rates persistently positive but moderate** (5–25 bp/8h)
- **Stablecoin supply expansion** (USDT/USDC issuance) matches risk-asset flows
- CEX leverage ratios rise
- DeFi TVL grows
- New leverage products launch (perp DEXes, lending protocols)
- Borrow rates on stablecoins compress as supply outruns demand

### Strategy implication
- Trend-following + breakout entries dominant
- [[trading/mechanisms/pivotal-points|Pivotal-point breakouts]] work cleanly with high confluence
- Position sizing can be aggressive within [[investment/allocation/equity-management-rules|equity-management rules]]
- Holding periods can be longer (sit-tight on confirmed breakouts)

## Stage 3 — Euphoria

**Description.** The transition from rational expansion to mania. "This time is different" rationalizations dominate. Outsiders pile in — people who were aloof from the asset class previously. Speculation is for capital gains, not income. Apparent leverage *falls* because asset values rise faster than debt — concealing real fragility.

> "There is nothing as disturbing to one's well-being and judgment as to see a friend get rich." — Kindleberger Ch 2

> "Authorities recognize that something exceptional is happening in the economy and while they are mindful of earlier manias, 'this time it's different', and they have extensive explanations for the difference." — Kindleberger Ch 2

### Telltale market signatures
- Parabolic price acceleration
- Velocity of trading rises (Ch 2: NYSE 1928 +36%/yr, first 8 months of 1929 +53%/yr; NASDAQ 1998 +41%/yr, then +101% over 15 months)
- Story stocks decouple from earnings
- New issues / IPOs surge
- Retail brokerage account openings spike
- "Cocktail-party indicator" — non-investors discussing the asset
- Authorities offer rationalizations (Greenspan's 1996/1997 productivity-surge thesis is canonical)

### Telltale credit/macro signatures
- Margin debt at multi-year highs
- New credit vehicles emerge
- "Innovative" structures appear (CDOs in 2000s, perp-DEXes + algorithmic stables in 2020s)
- Migration from hedge → speculative → Ponzi accelerates ([[foundation/market-wisdom/minsky-three-finance|see Minsky taxonomy]])

### Historical examples
- NYSE 1928–29
- Japan 1989
- Dot-com Q4'99–Q1'00
- US housing 2005–2007
- Crypto Nov 2021 (peak euphoria; see [[foundation/macro/crypto-2020-2022-cycle|the worked example]])

### Crypto carrier (current observable)
- **Funding rates >50bp/8h sustained** (annualized >50%)
- **Perp basis vs spot >10% annualized**
- **App store ranking** of crypto apps in top-10
- **Google Trends** "Bitcoin" / "Ethereum" / token names spike
- **Retail-cohort net inflows** on exchanges high
- **Long/short ratio extreme** on Binance/Bybit
- **New token issuance with no fundamentals** raising hundreds of millions
- **NFT floor prices going parabolic**
- **Influencer marketing peaks**
- **Levered ETPs see record inflows**

### Strategy implication
- Trend-following still works — **but with rising tail risk**
- Tighten stops; the depth of the next drawdown is increasing
- **Exit-by-mechanism, not by target** — when distribution signs emerge ([[foundation/market-wisdom/composite-operator|Wyckoff CO]] at distribution phase), exit even if your target hasn't been hit
- Avoid adding new long exposure on weakness — the next "buy the dip" may not bounce
- Cross-confirm entries against [[trading/mechanisms/group-leader-divergence|group-leader divergence]] watch — leader rotation often precedes broader rollover

## Stage 4 — Distress

**Description.** The transition from euphoria to crisis. Insider/outsider flow imbalance reaches its limit — outsider eagerness wanes faster than insider selling. Smart money exits while retail is still buying. The first cracks appear: a bank fails, a fund blows up, a fraud is revealed. Most participants are still in denial.

> "At every moment the purchases of real estate or stocks by the new investors or outsiders are necessarily balanced by sales by the insiders." — Kindleberger Ch 2

> "The specific signal that precipitates the crisis may be the failure of a bank or of a firm, the revelation of a swindle or defalcation by an investor who sought to escape distress by dishonest means, or a sharp fall in the price of a security or a commodity." — Kindleberger Ch 2

### Telltale market signatures
- **Volatility regime shift** — first sustained vol expansion in years
- **Breadth deteriorates**: fewer names making new highs even as index does
- **Correlations rise** (defensives stop diverging)
- **Bear market rallies of 15–25%** punctuate a downward trend (Tokyo 1990s had 6 such)
- **Smart-money positioning indicators** (CFTC, on-chain whale flows) diverge from price

### Telltale credit/macro signatures
- Spreads widen
- New issuance slows
- The most-aggressive lenders pull back
- First failures (often a peripheral firm with the Ponzi-finance signature)

### Historical examples
- 1907: Knickerbocker Trust failure
- 1929: September 5 Babson break, then October
- 1989: Japanese stock peak Dec 1989; real-estate slowdown 1990
- 2007: Bear Stearns hedge funds; BNP Paribas freezing redemptions
- 2021/22: Terra/UST collapse May 2022 → 3AC June 2022 (see [[foundation/macro/crypto-2020-2022-cycle|crypto worked example]])

### Crypto carrier (current observable)
- **Funding flips negative or volatile**
- **Aggregate OI starts falling on weakness** rather than rising
- **Stablecoin redemptions** (USDT/USDC supply contraction) appear
- **Whale wallets net send-to-exchange**
- **CEX outflows of BTC/ETH stop or reverse**
- **First peripheral protocol failure** (algorithmic stable, over-leveraged lending platform)
- **NFT volumes fall sharply while floor prices hold** ("denial" phase)

### Strategy implication
- **[[trading/mechanisms/group-leader-divergence|Group-leader divergence]]** shorts now structurally favored
- **Reaction-on-rally fades** — bear market rallies are tradable from the short side
- **Vol-expansion plays** — buying volatility (options) outperforms
- **Avoid long-side breakout chases** — false breakouts dominate
- **Sit-tight on shorts** through the bear market rallies (per [[foundation/market-wisdom/sit-tight-discipline|sit-tight discipline]])

## Stage 5 — Panic / Revulsion

**Description.** Door-shut panic. Realization spreads that only a few can exit at near-peak prices. Selling cascades. Liquidations. Sometimes orderly, sometimes degenerating to forced-selling spirals. Terminates only when (a) prices reach buy-the-rubble levels, (b) exchanges halt trading, or (c) credible LOLR action.

> "Revulsion and discredit may lead to panic (or as the Germans put it, *Torschlusspanik*, 'door-shut-panic') as investors crowd to get through the door before it slams shut." — Kindleberger Ch 2

> "The panic feeds on itself until prices have declined so far and have become so low that investors are tempted to buy the less liquid assets, or until trade in the assets is stopped... or a lender of last resort succeeds in convincing investors that money will be made available in the amounts needed." — Kindleberger Ch 2

### Telltale market signatures
- Vol spike
- **Correlation → 1 across "risk" assets**
- **Forced selling visible**: stop-loss clusters hit, then cleared
- **Bid-side liquidity vanishes briefly**
- Bottom-fishing emerges only after multiple capitulation events

### Telltale credit/macro signatures
- Funding markets seize
- Counterparty fear ("discredit")
- Banks pull lines
- Margin calls
- Forced asset sales propagate

### Historical examples
- Oct–Nov 1929
- Oct 1987 (Black Monday)
- Oct 2008 (Lehman week)
- March 2020 (COVID, brief but textbook)
- May–June 2022 + Nov 2022 FTX (crypto sequence; see [[foundation/macro/crypto-2020-2022-cycle]])

### Crypto carrier (current observable)
- **Liquidation cascades visible on Coinglass** — billions in liquidations in <24h
- **Funding rates deeply negative** as shorts pile on
- **Stablecoin de-pegs** (USDT to $0.95, DAI off-peg)
- **CEX withdrawal queues / proof-of-reserves panics**
- **Memecoin / alts down 70–90%** from highs
- **BTC dominance rises sharply** (flight from alt to BTC)
- **On-chain panic-send to exchange peaks then exhausts**
- **Open interest collapses to multi-month lows**

### Crypto-specific termination signal

Per [[foundation/sources/kindleberger-manias-panics-crashes|the Kindleberger source page]]: crypto has **no LOLR by design**. Panic terminations in crypto must come from price exhaustion or exchange halts, not central-bank rescue announcements. The operational signal is:
- Liquidation cascade exhaustion (Coinglass — peak liquidations followed by declining)
- Funding floor — funding deeply negative but no longer making new lows
- On-chain panic-send to exchange peaking then exhausting

This is operationalized in [[trading/mechanisms/accumulation-distribution|the accumulation-distribution page's spring setup]] — the canonical crypto Wyckoff translation.

### Strategy implication
- **Liquidation-cascade aftermath plays** ([[trading/mechanisms/accumulation-distribution|spring setup]]) become primary
- **Position size carefully** — drawdowns can be -40% in days
- **Long-side capitulation entry** on multi-channel confluence (cascade + funding floor + on-chain panic-send peak + spot recovery)
- **Don't catch falling knives** — wait for confluence; first dip rarely is the bottom
- Multi-week to multi-month patience for the bottom to form

## Cross-Stage Considerations

### How long does each stage last?

Per Ch 2's operational definition: **a bubble is an upward price movement over an extended period of fifteen to forty months that then implodes**. Within that:

| Stage | Typical duration |
|-------|-----------------|
| Displacement | 0 (an event) to 6 months (gradual unfolding) |
| Boom | 6–24 months |
| Euphoria | 6–18 months (the parabolic finish) |
| Distress | 3–9 months |
| Panic | 1–6 months (intense), with bear-market continuing for longer |

Total from displacement to panic-bottom: **24–48 months** is typical; outliers in either direction.

For crypto specifically: 4-year halving cycles correlate with this duration but the alignment isn't precise. The 2020 displacement → Nov 2021 euphoria peak → Nov 2022 panic bottom = ~30 months — consistent with K's 15–40 month bubble window.

### Stage transition signals (forward-looking)

The most-actionable transitions:

| From | To | Forward signal |
|------|-----|----------------|
| Displacement → Boom | Credit channel opens; first sustained price rise on volume |
| Boom → Euphoria | "This time is different" rationalizations from authorities; outsider entry visible (app rankings, retail brokerage); apparent leverage falls (debt/equity declining as asset values rise faster) |
| Euphoria → Distress | First peripheral failure (algorithmic stable, over-leveraged fund); breadth deteriorates; smart-money flow diverges from price; insider/outsider flow imbalance peaks |
| Distress → Panic | Sharp single-event trigger (bank failure, swindle revelation, sharp commodity move); funding markets seize; correlation → 1 |
| Panic → Recovery | Price exhaustion + LOLR (where available) + multi-channel confluence (cascade exhausted, funding floored, panic-send peaked) |

### Pace-asynchronization across markets

Per Ch 2's "International Propagation" section, bubbles propagate via four channels: arbitrage, trade, capital flows, **psychology** (the fastest). Black Monday 1987's near-instantaneous global decline demonstrates psychological transmission speed.

For crypto: psychological transmission is *more extreme* — Twitter / CT propagation is real-time, and a single tweet (CZ on FTX November 2022) can collapse a $30B exchange in 72 hours. The framework's "international propagation" applies even within crypto's internal sub-sectors (BTC → ETH → alts → memecoins → NFTs each have their own euphoria peaks but propagate sentiment across).

### Connection to other wiki concepts

- **Wyckoff [[foundation/market-wisdom/composite-operator|Composite Operator]] cycle** maps onto Kindleberger stages: CO accumulation = late-Distress / early-Recovery; CO markup = Boom; CO distribution = late-Euphoria / early-Distress; CO markdown = Distress → Panic.
- **Lynch's [[trading/mechanisms/cyclical-timing|cyclical-timing]]** is the per-asset version; Kindleberger is the whole-market version. Both diagnose phase position; Lynch via valuation (P/E inversion), Kindleberger via flow + credit + sentiment.
- **[[trading/mechanisms/accumulation-distribution|Wyckoff accumulation/distribution]]** mechanism is operational *within each Kindleberger stage* — there are mini-accumulations and mini-distributions throughout. The spring setup at end of Panic = the canonical Kindleberger-stage-5 long entry.
- **[[foundation/market-structure/bubbles-crashes-circuit-breakers|Harris's bubbles-crashes-circuit-breakers]]** page is the *microstructure* layer of the same phenomenon. Harris analyzes the 1987 cascade via portfolio insurance dynamics; Kindleberger analyzes the same period via credit-cycle and bubble-stage. They compose: Harris tells you the *mechanics* of a cascade once it starts; Kindleberger tells you *which stage* you're in (and thus whether a cascade is coming).

## Honest Caveats

- **Doesn't predict timing.** The framework identifies which stage we're *in*; it cannot predict when the next stage transition occurs. "When does Euphoria flip to Distress?" is outside the framework's scope.
- **Doesn't predict magnitude.** "Panic produces sharp price decline" — but -30%, -60%, or -90%? Calibration is per-asset-class. Crypto's no-LOLR architecture amplifies drawdowns vs equities.
- **Hindsight bias risk.** Without ex-ante stage calls documented, the framework becomes a retrospective ornament. **Discipline required**: stage assignments must be made *contemporaneously*, written down at the time, with ex-ante calls vs ex-post outcomes tracked honestly. Per Quality Standards.
- **Sub-cycles and noise.** Within a multi-year cycle, sub-cycles exist (mini-bubbles in specific tokens during a broader Boom). The framework applies at multiple timescales but stage labels may differ across timescales for the same date.
- **Crypto's interconnected cycles.** Each crypto cycle inherits institutions, leverage stacks, and counterparty exposures from the prior cycle (Mt. Gox claims; FTX bankruptcy ongoing). This continuity may affect stage-transition signals between cycles.
- **The framework isn't quantitative.** "Funding >50bp/8h sustained" as a Euphoria signature is calibrated against past cycles; future cycles may shift the threshold. Empirical re-calibration each cycle is required.
- **Self-defeating risk.** As more traders use the framework, the contrarian edge of "I see we're in Euphoria; I'll exit" gets crowded. The framework's value may compress over time as more practitioners apply it.

## Key Takeaways

- 5-stage cycle: Displacement → Boom → Euphoria → Distress → Panic/Revulsion. Structural fidelity across 400 years.
- The **regime-conditioning** value is the load-bearing operational use: each stage has different optimal strategies.
- **Boom**: trend-following + breakouts.
- **Euphoria**: trend-following with tightening stops; alert for distribution.
- **Distress**: group-leader-divergence shorts; vol-expansion; reaction-on-rally fades.
- **Panic**: liquidation-cascade aftermath ([[trading/mechanisms/accumulation-distribution|spring]]); long-side capitulation entries.
- Crypto exhibits the framework with **better instrumentation** than 1978 (stablecoin supply + perp OI + funding + on-chain whale flow + Coinglass liquidation maps).
- Crypto has **no LOLR** — termination signals are price exhaustion + halts, not central-bank rescue.
- Stage transition signals are forward-looking (specific signatures listed above), but **timing of transitions** is outside the framework's predictive scope.
- Hindsight bias risk requires contemporaneous stage assignment with documented predictions.
- Sub-cycles can exist within larger cycles; multi-timescale stage analysis required.

## Hayes contemporary calibration — Multi-currency credit-impulse refinement

Per [[foundation/sources/hayes-essays|Hayes Substack ingest 2026-05-08]], "Long Live the King!" (Oct 2025) refines Stage 2 (Boom / Credit Expansion) with a **multi-currency observation**: BTC cycles respond to whichever monetary system is expanding most aggressively at the time, not exclusively to USD.

Specific Hayes calibration anchors:

- **2013 Genesis cycle**: USD-driven ("The supply of dollars rose sharply and peaked and rolled over at the end of 2013")
- **2015-17 cycle**: yuan-driven ("Bitcoin skyrocketed because of a fuck ton of yuan sloshing around the global money markets") — Chinese credit impulse acceleration ahead of Fed expansion
- **2024-26 current cycle**: simultaneous USD + yuan easing, which is why the four-year halving cycle "fails" to produce a clear bear-phase trigger

**Stage-2 framework refinement**: rather than asking "is USD credit expanding?", ask "is the BTC-dominant monetary system's credit expanding?" The dominant system can rotate (USD → CNY → USD again). Forward-looking implication: Stage-4 (Distress) onset should track whichever monetary system was the cycle's dominant expander; if it begins tightening while another system loosens, the cycle may extend rather than terminate.

This is genuinely additive vs the existing Kindleberger / Minsky framework — the original framework was developed in the gold-standard / Bretton-Woods era when one monetary system dominated; Hayes's multi-currency lens fits the post-2008 dual-reserve world.
