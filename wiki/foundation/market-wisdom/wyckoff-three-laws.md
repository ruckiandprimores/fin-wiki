# The Three Laws of Wyckoff — Supply/Demand, Cause/Effect, Effort vs. Result

> **TL;DR**: The diagnostic framework for reading what the [[foundation/market-wisdom/composite-operator|Composite Operator]] is doing. Three laws — (1) **Supply and Demand** determines direction; (2) **Cause and Effect** determines magnitude (proxied via P&F horizontal count); (3) **Effort vs. Result** diagnoses genuineness (volume = effort, price change = result; mismatch reveals informed counterparty). **Honest attribution caveat**: Wyckoff names Supply/Demand explicitly in the primary 1910/1931 texts; Cause/Effect and Effort vs. Result are *operationally articulated* by Wyckoff but the *named formal trinity* was canonized by [[foundation/sources/wyckoff-method|successor teachers]] (Evans, Pruden, Weis, Fraser). The wiki uses the formal trinity because it's clearer pedagogy, while flagging which parts are primary Wyckoff vs. successor synthesis.

## Why The Three Laws Matter

[[foundation/market-wisdom/composite-operator|The Composite Operator framing]] gives the trader a *who* and *what*: aggregate large-interest flow acting in a four-phase cycle. The Three Laws give the trader the *diagnostic procedure* — how to read what the CO is doing in real time, from price and volume alone.

Together they form the operational core of Wyckoff: the CO frames *what to look for*; the Three Laws specify *how to look*.

The three laws answer three different questions about every market move:

| Law | Question it answers | Operational diagnostic |
|-----|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Supply and Demand** | Which side has the weight right now? | Wave Chart; bid-ask volume scale; absorption observation |
| **Cause and Effect** | How big is the eventual move likely to be? | P&F horizontal count of the accumulation/distribution range |
| **Effort vs. Result** | Is the current move genuine, or is someone large absorbing/distributing on the other side? | Compare volume (effort) to price change (result); mismatch reveals counterparty |

## Law 1 — Supply and Demand (Direction)

The fundamental law. Wyckoff names it explicitly and repeatedly across both primary texts.

### Wyckoff's articulation

> "Your trading operations are based, not on what you formerly regarded as the market's characteristics but on the fundamental law of supply and demand, which is at the bottom of every move that is made in every stock in the market at all times. This law is working and will continue to work always and forever. There can be no getting away from it. **It does not matter whether the buying and the selling, or both, are genuine or artificial; that is, manipulative.**" (Method TR Sec 10)

> "This market price is like a pair of scales, and the volume of stock thrown by sellers and reached for by purchasers shows toward which side the preponderance of weight has momentarily shifted." (Studies Ch V)

### The mechanism

Direction is determined by which side has the heavier weight of orders right now. Manipulation does not invalidate the law; manipulation *implements* it. A manipulator who wants to bull a stock must produce more buying than selling at his target level — which is precisely what the law requires anyway.

This is the load-bearing reason Wyckoff treats manipulation as *signal*, not noise. The CO must obey supply/demand to operate; therefore his actions are visible through the same mechanism that any large flow is visible.

### Operational diagnostic

- **Wave Chart**: aggregate-of-leaders proxy. Compare buying waves to selling waves — whichever travels longer in distance and time has the weight.
- **Tape Reading Chart**: 1/8-point figure chart with volume at each price; reveals where transactions clustered.
- **Bid-ask scale**: where size lots clump determines weight. Wyckoff's worked example (Studies Ch V): `500 . 50 . 1000 . ⅛ . 50 . 1500 . ⅛` = 700 sold at 50 vs. 2,500 taken at 50⅛ → buying side heavier → next print likely higher.
- **Absorption observation**: persistent volume at a level *without* price advance = informed counterparty selling into the buying. The Reading 50,000-share churn at 144 (Studies Ch V) is the canonical example.

### Plain-language summary

The market price always reflects which side currently has more weight. Watch where size shows up, watch which side absorbs, watch how price responds to attempted moves — that's the supply-demand reading. Manipulation doesn't break the law; it operates through it.

## Law 2 — Cause and Effect (Magnitude)

The law of *how big* the next move is likely to be, proxied via the width of the accumulation/distribution range.

### Wyckoff's articulation

The *operational logic* is in primary Wyckoff (Studies Ch VIII):

> "Some people claim to be able to predict how far such a movement will go by counting the number of full figures which form an unbroken line. In a case where a stock shows a long horizontal line of 19s and 20s at the bottom of a decline, and a count of the 20s shows that there are twelve of them, this is taken as an indication that there will be a rise of twelve points from 20."

> "It is logical to suppose that the more important the contemplated move, the more stock will the insiders wish to buy and the longer time will be required in which to accumulate it." (Studies Ch VIII)

The *naming as "Law of Cause and Effect"* is successor synthesis (Evans, Pruden). The principle is primary Wyckoff; the formal label is post-1934.

### The mechanism

The longer/wider the accumulation phase, the larger the line accumulated, the larger the move when markup begins. Same in inverse for distribution → markdown. Cause = the time/range spent accumulating or distributing; Effect = the magnitude of the resulting move.

Wyckoff's reasoning: a CO accumulating a large line cannot do so quickly without revealing his intent. A larger intended move requires a larger line, which requires a longer accumulation. The accumulation footprint *is* the size-of-intended-move signal.

### Operational diagnostic

- **P&F horizontal count**: count the number of P&F columns within the trading range; project the count from the breakout level. A 12-column horizontal line at 20 → 12-point projected move from 20 (i.e., 32).
- **Range-width-to-move ratio**: empirical adjustment for asset volatility — a 12-point range in a low-vol stock projects differently than the same range in a high-vol stock.
- **Range-duration assessment**: longer time in range = larger expected move; same width, different durations imply different cause sizes.

### Plain-language summary

Big moves come out of big bases. Small moves come out of small bases. The accumulation/distribution phase *sizes* the eventual breakout. The P&F horizontal count is Wyckoff's specific tool; the principle generalizes to any range-width-vs-move-magnitude analysis.

## Law 3 — Effort vs. Result (Genuineness)

The law of *whether* the current move is genuine or has hidden counterparty absorption/distribution.

### Wyckoff's articulation

The *operational logic* permeates both primary texts:

> "Notwithstanding the predominance of apparent demand, the resistance offered (whether legitimate or artificial) became too great for the stock to overcome, and it fell back from 144⅛… On the way up these volumes alone suggested a purchase, but the tape showed abnormal transactions accompanied by poor response from the rest of the list. This suggested manipulation and warned the operator to be cautious on the bull side." (Studies Ch V)

> "It required 8400 shares to be taken in order to advance the stock 1¼ points. This is not bullish because of the way they did it; therefore, bearish." (Method TR Sec 5)

> "Activity fails to increase on the down side, however, and a tentatively bullish signal is given by the evidence of support around the morning low." (Method TR Sec 2-A)

The *naming as "Law of Effort vs. Result"* is successor synthesis. The principle is primary Wyckoff at the operational level.

### The mechanism

Volume is the *effort* expended by buyers and sellers; price change is the *result*. When effort doesn't translate into commensurate result, someone large is absorbing on the other side.

Patterns:

| Effort | Result | Diagnosis |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **High volume, small price advance** | Mismatch | Hidden seller absorbing demand → bearish |
| **High volume, small price decline** | Mismatch | Hidden buyer absorbing supply → bullish |
| **High volume, large price move with the trend** | Match | Genuine breakout/breakdown — trend continuation |
| **Low volume, large price move** | Mismatch (different way) | Lack of commitment; suspect; prone to reversal |
| **Low volume, small price decline against trend** | Match | No real selling pressure; bullish (lack of supply confirmation) |

### Operational diagnostic

- **Per-bar volume vs. price change**: the canonical effort/result check.
- **CVD divergence (crypto carrier)**: cumulative volume delta diverging from price = effort and result mismatched.
- **Wyckoff's 8400-shares-for-1¼-points example** (Method TR Sec 5): effort large, result small, contrary to the apparent direction — bearish despite the price tick up.
- **Three-stage check**: does effort precede result (accumulation → markup), does result diverge from effort mid-trend (distribution begins), does effort fail to produce result at the extreme (exhaustion)?

### Plain-language summary

Volume is what the participants are *trying* to do; price change is what they *succeeded* in doing. When trying doesn't succeed, someone is on the other side preventing it — and that someone is the informed counterparty whose side you want to be on.

## Using The Three Laws Together

The laws are *sequential* in the diagnostic procedure:

1. **Supply and Demand** first — establish direction. Which side has weight *right now*?
2. **Effort vs. Result** second — verify genuineness. Is the apparent direction real, or is something hidden?
3. **Cause and Effect** third — size the trade. If the move is genuine, how far is it likely to go (using the width of the accumulation/distribution range)?

Worked example using primary-Wyckoff pattern from Studies Ch V (the 1909 Reading distribution):

1. **Supply/Demand reading**: 50,000 shares change hands at 144 in a 3/4-point band. Apparent demand (price holding at 144). But —
2. **Effort vs. Result**: high effort (50K shares), small result (3/4-pt range). Mismatch → hidden distribution. Confirmed by lack of follow-through in the rest of the list.
3. **Cause and Effect**: small cause (narrow distribution range, short time) → small effect expected. Wyckoff sells short; rides break to lower prices.

This is the canonical Wyckoff diagnostic in three steps.

## Crypto Application

The three laws translate directly. Crypto's instrumentation is *better* than Wyckoff had — the same diagnostic procedure, with more visible inputs.

| Law | Crypto carrier (observable today) |
|-----|------------------------------------|
| **Supply and Demand** | Aggressor flow (taker buy vs. taker sell ratio); CVD; on-chain whale flow direction; order-book absorption visible at each level |
| **Cause and Effect** | Range-width-to-move ratio empirically; OI buildup during range as a "size of cause" proxy; multi-day consolidation duration |
| **Effort vs. Result** | CVD divergence from price; on-chain volume vs. price action; high-OI moves vs. low-OI moves; volume + funding + OI + price across the four channels |

For the central catalog of these carriers, see [[foundation/market-structure/crypto-carrier-features]].

### Specific crypto-native effort/result patterns

- **Funding-rate divergence**: persistent positive funding (effort: longs pay rent) without commensurate price advance (result: price flat) = hidden distribution. The trader's a funding-carry strategy systematically exploits the structural-funding component this reveals.
- **OI-volume mismatch**: rising OI without rising price = positions opening but not winning (effort/result mismatch); informed shorts often on the other side.
- **On-chain transfer / spot-price mismatch**: large whale transfers to exchanges (effort to sell visible) while spot price holds (result of selling absorbed) = informed buyer on the other side; bullish.
- **Liquidation cascade with quick recovery**: cascade wick (high effort selling forced) with immediate recovery (result not sustained) = absorption / spring setup.

## Honest Caveats

- **Successor synthesis caveat (most important).** "Cause and Effect" and "Effort vs. Result" as *named laws* are post-Wyckoff (Evans, 1940s+). The *operational principles* are primary Wyckoff. The wiki uses the named trinity for clarity but flags the attribution. Per [[foundation/sources/wyckoff-method|the source page]] honest-caveats section.
- **Three Laws ≠ Three Indicators.** The laws are diagnostic frameworks, not signals. They tell you what to look for; they don't fire entry/exit triggers automatically.
- **The Effort vs. Result check is hardest in real time.** It requires a calibrated sense of "normal" volume-to-price ratio for the specific asset and regime. New traders confuse "high volume" (in absolute terms) with "high effort" (relative to recent baselines).
- **Cause and Effect's P&F count is empirically loose in crypto.** The P&F horizontal count was designed for 1910 NYSE block sizes and tick conventions. Modern crypto has different volatility regimes; the projection ratio should be empirically calibrated, not borrowed wholesale.
- **Manipulation can fake all three.** A sophisticated manipulator can produce high-volume tape with structured price action that *looks* like accumulation when it's distribution, or vice versa. Wyckoff's defense was the multi-channel check (Wave Chart + Tape Reading Chart + group breadth + sector behavior). In crypto: cross-confirm with on-chain flow, funding, OI, liquidation maps. No single channel is sufficient.
- **The laws assume rational coherent flow.** During regime transitions (Fed pivot, ETF launch, banking crisis, war), aggregate flow becomes incoherent for a period — the three laws give noisy readings until the new regime stabilizes.

## Key Takeaways

- Three laws answer three different questions: direction (Supply/Demand), magnitude (Cause/Effect), genuineness (Effort vs. Result).
- Supply/Demand is named explicitly in primary Wyckoff; Cause/Effect and Effort vs. Result are operationally present but the formal naming is successor synthesis.
- The diagnostic procedure is sequential: direction → genuineness → magnitude.
- Manipulation does not invalidate the laws; manipulation operates through them, leaving footprints.
- Crypto instrumentation (CVD, on-chain whale flow, funding, OI, liquidation maps) makes the three-law diagnostic *more observable* than in 1910.
- Multi-channel confirmation is essential — no single channel is sufficient for any of the three laws.
- The Cause and Effect P&F count needs empirical calibration for crypto's different volatility regime.
