# Crypto 2020-2022 Cycle — Kindleberger-Minsky Framework Applied

> **TL;DR**: A worked example mapping the [[foundation/market-wisdom/bubble-cycle-stages|5-stage Kindleberger framework]] onto the most-recent fully-completed crypto cycle. Displacement Q1-Q2 2020 (COVID Fed action + DeFi Summer + retail brokerage explosion). Boom Q3 2020 - Q4 2021. Euphoria peak ~Nov 2021 ($69K BTC, $4,800 ETH). Distress Q1-Q2 2022 (Terra/UST May; 3AC June). Panic Nov 2022 (FTX collapse; -77% BTC peak-to-trough). **No LOLR**: cycle terminated on price exhaustion + macro tailwind, not rescue. **Ponzi-finance signatures** were openly visible in real time (Anchor 19.5% APY; 3AC GBTC/stETH/Luna stack; CeFi yield platforms). Cycle duration from displacement to bottom: ~30 months — within Kindleberger's 15–40 month bubble window.

## Why This Page Exists

The [[foundation/sources/kindleberger-manias-panics-crashes|Kindleberger framework]] is descriptive — it categorizes cycles after the fact. Its operational value depends on **whether stage signatures can be identified contemporaneously**, not just retrospectively. This page is the wiki's first worked example: applying the framework, with full per-stage signatures, to a cycle the trader actually traded.

Three uses:

1. **Calibration reference.** Future cycles can be compared against this one — were the Euphoria signatures of Cycle N similar in pattern (if not in absolute level) to those of 2021? This is the empirical grounding the framework needs.
2. **Salvage path for retrospective lessons.** Specific positions and protocols that failed (Anchor, 3AC, FTX, Celsius) are catalogued with the *signature visible at the time*, not just post-mortem.
3. **Forward-looking signature templates.** What did "we are now in Distress" look like in May 2022? These templates inform live stage-classification in the current cycle.

This page is **not** a complete crypto market history. The full multi-cycle reference is at **[[foundation/macro/btc-regime-taxonomy-2020-2025]]** (created 2026-05-04 — completes tier-list item #4). This page is a single-cycle deep dive applying the framework; the regime taxonomy extends the timeline to 2025/2026 with operational marker tables.

## Stage 1 — Displacement (Q1–Q2 2020)

**Multiple compounding shocks created the displacement, not a single event.** Crypto was uniquely positioned to absorb each:

### The COVID Fed action (March 2020)

- **Fed rate cut to 0–0.25%** (March 15, 2020).
- **Unlimited QE** (March 23, 2020): "to support smooth market functioning."
- **CARES Act stimulus** (March 27, 2020): $2.2T including direct retail payments.
- **M2 money supply** grew ~25% from Feb 2020 to Feb 2021 — the largest one-year expansion in modern US history.
- **Asset-price reflation**: nearly all financial assets bid up; crypto was a marginal beneficiary as a high-beta risk asset.

### DeFi Summer (June – September 2020)

- **June 15, 2020**: Compound launches COMP token with **liquidity mining** — depositors earn COMP tokens for participating. Within a week, COMP rose from launch to >$300, and TVL on Compound went from ~$100M to ~$700M.
- **September 2020**: Uniswap retroactively airdrops UNI (400 UNI to every wallet that ever used the protocol — ~$1,200 at launch, eventually >$20,000 at peak).
- **Total DeFi TVL**: ~$1B (Jan 2020) → ~$15B (Sep 2020) → ~$80B (May 2021).
- **New mechanism**: yield farming as a cash-on-cash return generator outside traditional finance.

### Retail brokerage explosion

- **Robinhood** crypto trading enabled in additional states; user base grew from ~10M (early 2020) to >22M (mid 2021).
- **Coinbase retail accounts** grew from ~35M (2020) to ~73M (Q2 2021); IPO April 2021 at $250 (~$76B valuation peak).
- **Lockdown effect**: retail traders home, with stimulus money, and apps making crypto exposure trivially accessible.

### Institutional adoption signals

- **MicroStrategy first BTC purchase**: Aug 11, 2020 ($425M, 21,454 BTC).
- **Square (Block) BTC purchase**: Oct 8, 2020 ($50M).
- **Tesla BTC purchase**: Feb 8, 2021 ($1.5B).
- **Coinbase IPO**: Apr 14, 2021.
- **First Bitcoin futures ETF (BITO)**: Oct 19, 2021.

### NFT proof-of-concept

- **CryptoPunks** (originally 2017) saw renewed interest.
- **NBA Top Shot** launched Sept 2020; viral by Q1 2021.
- **Bored Apes** mint Apr 23, 2021.
- **Beeple's "Everydays"** sold for $69M at Christie's, March 11, 2021.

### Was the displacement "real"?

Yes — multiple genuine economic mechanisms were created:
- DeFi liquidity provision is a real new financial primitive (subsequently survived the bear market and continues to operate).
- Bitcoin as a Treasury asset for inflation-anxious corporates is a real (if controversial) thesis.
- NFT marketplaces created new property rights mechanisms (subsequently atrophied but didn't die).
- The retail-brokerage UX revolution genuinely lowered barriers to entry.

**The displacement being real is what made the bubble possible.** Bubbles need real seeds. The 2020 displacement was unusually *thick* — multiple compounding shocks rather than one — which contributed to the cycle's amplitude.

### Position in the framework

Per Kindleberger Ch 2: "If the shock is sufficiently large and pervasive, the anticipated profit opportunities improve in at least one important sector of the economy: the profit share of GDP increases."

The 2020 displacement was *exceptionally large and pervasive* — the largest displacement in crypto's history to that point. The Boom phase that followed was correspondingly large.

## Stage 2 — Boom / Credit Expansion (Q3 2020 – Q4 2021)

**Credit accommodated the displacement.** Crypto credit takes forms TradFi credit doesn't, and several were activated simultaneously.

### The crypto credit channels

| Channel | What it is | Q1 2020 → Q4 2021 growth |
|---------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Stablecoin supply** | USD-pegged tokens (USDT, USDC, BUSD, DAI) functioning as M0 for the crypto economy | USDT: ~$5B → ~$78B (Q1 2022). USDC: ~$0.5B → ~$50B. **Aggregate stablecoin supply grew ~15x.** |
| **Perp open interest** | Aggregate notional of open perpetual futures positions | BTC perp OI: ~$2B → ~$24B. Aggregate crypto perp OI: ~$5B → ~$80B+. |
| **CEX leverage** | Margin accounts on centralized exchanges | Long/short ratios persistently long-skewed. Average BTC leverage on offered exchange products rose from ~5× to ~50× retail-accessible. |
| **DeFi lending TVL** | Funds deposited as collateral to borrow against | Aave + Compound + others: ~$1B → ~$50B. |
| **CeFi yield platforms** | Retail-deposit centralized yield products (Celsius, BlockFi, Voyager, Gemini Earn) | $0 → ~$50B in retail deposits at peak. |

The aggregate "credit" deployed into crypto risk assets grew from ~$10B (Q1 2020) to ~$300B+ (Q4 2021) across these channels — **a 30× expansion in 21 months**, significantly faster than asset prices grew (~10×).

### "Credit ahead of price" is the textbook Boom signature

Per Kindleberger Ch 2: "The boom in the Minsky model is fueled by an expansion of credit." When credit grows faster than price, the leverage ratio rises and the system's *latent fragility* increases — even if prices look "rational" relative to credit availability.

### Boom market signatures observed

- **BTC**: $9K (Sept 1, 2020) → $64K (Apr 14, 2021) → consolidation $30K-$50K → $69K (Nov 10, 2021).
- **ETH**: $400 → $4,200 → $1,800 (June 2021 dip) → $4,800 (Nov 10, 2021).
- **DeFi tokens**: AAVE $30 → $660; SUSHI $0.50 → $24; YFI $0 (launched Jul 2020) → $94K.
- **Drawdowns shallow and bought**: every dip <30% recovered to new highs within weeks (until Q4 2021).
- **Volatility low for crypto**: realized 30-day vol on BTC averaged ~50–70% during Boom (vs >100% during the prior 2018 bear).
- **New entrants reporting strong returns**: every major financial publication had a "I made X% on crypto" story by mid-2021.

### Boom credit signatures observed

- **Stablecoin yield wars**: Anchor Protocol (Terra) at 19.5% APY on UST; Celsius at 8–18% APY on stables; BlockFi at 7–9%.
- **DeFi yield farming returns**: 50%+ APY common on Compound/Aave for short windows; thousands of percent APY on early-stage farms.
- **Perp funding moderate**: average BTC perp funding ~5–25 bp / 8h during 2021 — *positive but not extreme* for most of the Boom.
- **New leverage products**: GMX (Sept 2021) launched perp DEX; Anchor's UST yield mechanism; rehypothecation by CeFi platforms.

### When did Boom transition to Euphoria?

The transition is fuzzy by design — but in retrospect, several signals point to roughly **Q1 2021** as the turn:
- BTC's first parabolic acceleration ($30K → $60K in 8 weeks, Jan-Apr 2021).
- DOGE's first parabolic move ($0.005 → $0.45 by April 2021).
- Beeple's $69M NFT sale (March 11, 2021).
- "Cocktail-party indicator" — non-investor friends asking about crypto for the first time.

A more conservative reading puts the Euphoria transition at Q3 2021 (BTC's second parabola from $30K → $69K). The wiki's position: **both Q1 2021 and Q3 2021 had Euphoria-quality signatures; the cycle had two euphoric peaks separated by a Distress-like cooling-off in summer 2021** (the China mining ban May 2021 broke the first parabola; recovery into a new euphoria peak is a bubble-cycle anomaly).

## Stage 3 — Euphoria (Q1–Q4 2021, with peak Nov 2021)

**The transition from rational expansion to mania.** "This time is different" rationalizations dominated. Outsiders piled in. Apparent leverage *fell* (asset values rose faster than debt) — concealing the rising real fragility.

### Euphoria market signatures

- **BTC parabolic moves**: $30K → $64K (Jan-Apr 2021); $30K → $69K (Jul-Nov 2021).
- **ETH parabolic**: $1,800 → $4,800 (Jul-Nov 2021).
- **Memecoin parabolic**: DOGE $0.005 → $0.74 (May 2021); Shiba Inu $0 (launched Aug 2020) → $0.000088 (Oct 2021, ~10,000,000% gain).
- **NFT mania**: Beeple $69M; CryptoPunks floor $400 (Jan 2021) → $470,000 (Aug 2021); BAYC mints + derivative collections.
- **"Right-click-save" rejected as outdated thinking**.
- **Retail entry signatures**:
  - Coinbase app #1 in App Store: May 2021.
  - Crypto.com Super Bowl ad with Matt Damon: February 2022.
  - FTX Super Bowl ad with Larry David: February 2022.
  - FTX naming rights for Miami Heat arena: $135M, 19-year deal: April 2021.
  - Trade volume on Robinhood crypto trades exceeded equities for stretches in May 2021.
- **"This time is different" rationalizations**:
  - "Institutional adoption thesis"
  - "Fixed supply hedge against inflation"
  - "DeFi will replace banking"
  - "NFTs are a new asset class"
  - "The metaverse is the future of work"
  - "Web3 will replace Web2"
- **Authorities rationalize**: SEC (Gensler) ambivalent rather than prohibitive; Fed (Powell) acknowledging Bitcoin as "speculative store of value" rather than dismissing.

### Euphoria credit signatures

- **Funding rates >50bp/8h sustained**: average BTC perp funding during Q4 2021 ~30–80 bp per 8h period — annualized 100–250%.
- **Perp basis vs spot >10% annualized**: futures premium reached extreme levels (CME basis trade was a dominant institutional position).
- **Stablecoin yield wars** at 19.5% APY (Anchor) — *unsustainable by construction*, openly described as Ponzi-finance even by sympathetic observers.
- **Lending platform borrow rates near zero on stables** — supply outran demand at non-extreme rates.
- **3AC reportedly running ~$10B AUM with massive lending leverage** — borrowing from BlockFi, Genesis, Celsius, Voyager simultaneously, with collateral including positions that depended on continued price appreciation (GBTC, stETH, LUNA).

### The hidden Ponzi finance regime

Per [[foundation/market-wisdom/minsky-three-finance|Minsky three-finance taxonomy]], by Q4 2021 the crypto system had a *large Ponzi share* — borrowers/positions whose debt service depended on continued asset appreciation:

- **Anchor Protocol's 19.5% UST yield**: paid from a reserve that depended on continued UST minting, which depended on LUNA price appreciation. Pure Ponzi, openly so.
- **Token-collateralized loans** (3AC, others): collateral asset value was *endogenous* to the borrowing activity — when the activity stopped, collateral fell, loans defaulted.
- **CeFi yield platforms** (Celsius, BlockFi, Voyager): paying retail 8–18% on stables, generating returns from a mix of risky proprietary trading and *new deposit inflows*. Textbook Ponzi when the deposit flow reverses.
- **Algorithmic stable-supply mechanisms** (UST, FRAX in part, Iron Finance): supply mechanisms that required continued demand to maintain peg — circular.
- **Token treasuries paying operational costs by selling more tokens**: standard practice for many DeFi protocols. Sustainable in Boom, fatal in Distress.

The "where does the cash come from?" diagnostic could be applied in real time to all of the above; the Ponzi structure was *visible* at the time. The wiki's claim is not "we should have known" but "we *did* know if we asked the question — and most participants didn't ask."

### Euphoria peak: November 10, 2021

- **BTC**: $69,044 high.
- **ETH**: $4,891 high.
- **Total crypto market cap**: ~$3T peak (Nov 9, 2021).
- **BTC dominance** at this peak: ~42% (alts had outperformed BTC throughout the latter Boom).
- **Funding rates**: extreme positive — annualized 200%+ on aggregate.
- **Open interest**: aggregate ~$80B+, BTC perp OI ~$24B.
- **Stablecoin supply**: USDT ~$74B, USDC ~$45B.

After this peak, BTC rolled over without making a new high. The signal was visible by January 2022 — but most participants still believed in a "santa rally" or "spring 2022 continuation."

## Stage 4 — Distress (Q1–Q3 2022)

**The transition from euphoria to crisis.** Insider/outsider flow imbalance reached its limit. Smart money exited while retail was still buying. The first cracks appeared. Most participants were in denial, with bear-market rallies sustaining the "this is just a correction" narrative.

### Macro pivot triggers Distress

- **December 2021**: Fed signals tightening (faster taper, rate hike sequence anticipated).
- **March 16, 2022**: First Fed rate hike (25bp). Risk-off begins in earnest.
- **BTC**: $69K (Nov 2021) → $48K (Jan 2022) → $40K (Feb 2022) → $29K (May 2022).

The macro pivot is *the trigger*, not the cause. The cause was the latent Ponzi-finance composition built up during Euphoria. The trigger could have been almost anything that constricted credit; macro pivot happened to be it.

### First peripheral failures (the canonical Distress signature)

Per Kindleberger Ch 2: "The specific signal that precipitates the crisis may be the failure of a bank or of a firm, the revelation of a swindle or defalcation by an investor who sought to escape distress by dishonest means, or a sharp fall in the price of a security or a commodity."

The 2022 sequence:

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **Q1 2022** | First cracks in algorithmic stable / DeFi yield mechanisms; Anchor reserve drains | Peripheral protocol stress; ignored by most |
| **May 7-12, 2022** | **Terra/UST collapse** | $40B wiped out in a week. UST → $0.01, LUNA → $0.0001. Stage 4 trigger event — the first major failure. |
| **June 12, 2022** | **Celsius halts withdrawals** | First major CeFi failure. ~$12B in customer deposits. |
| **June 15-16, 2022** | **Three Arrows Capital insolvent** | ~$10B AUM hedge fund. GBTC + stETH + LUNA exposure. Forced selling cascades. Confirmed Luna loss alone: $200M. |
| **July 5, 2022** | **Voyager files Chapter 11** | Customer funds frozen. |
| **July 13, 2022** | **Celsius files Chapter 11** |
| **August 2022** | BlockFi gets FTX line of credit (which would later prove worthless) |

### Distress market signatures observed

- **Volatility regime shift**: BTC realized 30-day vol surged from ~50% (Boom average) to >100% during Q2 2022.
- **Breadth deterioration**: in Q4 2021 most majors made highs together; by Q1 2022 only BTC retested highs while ETH and alts already in clear downtrend.
- **Bear-market rallies**: BTC bounced from $17.5K (June 2022 panic-low) → $25K (August 2022) → fell again to $19K (Sept 2022). Classic distress signature: not yet final panic, denial alternating with capitulation.
- **Smart-money positioning indicators diverged**: on-chain whale-cohort net send-to-exchange spiked in Q1 2022 (whales selling) while retail was still buying the "cheap" prices.

### Distress credit signatures observed

- **Funding flipped negative or volatile**: funding went from extreme positive Q4 2021 to flat/negative by Q2 2022.
- **Aggregate OI fell on weakness**: positions closing rather than rolling — the structural Distress signal.
- **Stablecoin contraction**: USDT supply fell from $83B (May 2022) to $66B (Aug 2022) — retail and institutional withdrawals.
- **Whale wallets net send-to-exchange**: continuous distribution visible on-chain.
- **CEX outflows of BTC/ETH stopped or reversed**: prior pattern of "buying with conviction = withdraw to cold storage" reversed.

### The "denial phase" was visible

NFT volumes fell sharply during Q1 2022 *while floor prices held* — the canonical "denial" signature. Holders wouldn't sell at a loss; buyers wouldn't pay full price; volume collapsed. Eventually floor prices broke (NFT market never recovered to 2021 levels).

This was a textbook Kindleberger-Ch-2 distress observation:

> "The borrowers can only meet their commitments to pay the high interest rates on their outstanding loans or deposits if they obtain the cash from new loans or deposits."

When the inflows stopped, the dependent positions could not be sustained, but participants believed temporarily that the inflows would resume.

## Stage 5 — Panic / Revulsion (November 2022)

**Door-shut panic.** Realization spread that only a few could exit at near-peak prices. Selling cascaded. Liquidations. No LOLR rescue.

### The FTX collapse, November 8-11, 2022

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **Nov 2, 2022** | CoinDesk publishes report on Alameda Research balance sheet showing ~40% of assets in FTT (FTX's own token) |
| **Nov 6, 2022** | CZ (Binance CEO) tweets out: "As part of Binance's exit from FTX equity last year, Binance received roughly $2.1 billion USD equivalent in cash and FTT. Due to recent revelations that have come to light, we have decided to liquidate any remaining FTT on our books." |
| **Nov 7, 2022** | Bank run on FTX. Withdrawals exceed $5B in 24 hours. FTT crashes from $22 to $5. |
| **Nov 8, 2022** | FTX halts withdrawals. SBF announces Binance has agreed (non-binding) to acquire FTX.com (international). |
| **Nov 9, 2022** | Binance walks away from acquisition citing "issues beyond our ability or control." |
| **Nov 10, 2022** | Wash of contagion: BlockFi, Genesis, DCG troubles surface. |
| **Nov 11, 2022** | FTX, Alameda, and ~130 affiliated entities file Chapter 11. |
| **Nov 21, 2022** | **BTC bottom: $15,476**. Down 77.6% from $69K peak. |

### Contagion second wave (Nov 2022 - Q1 2023)

- **BlockFi bankruptcy**: Nov 28, 2022.
- **Genesis Global Trading**: pauses withdrawals Nov 16, 2022; ultimately bankruptcy Jan 2023.
- **DCG (Genesis parent) troubles**: ongoing through Q1 2023.
- **Gemini Earn frozen**: Nov 16, 2022.
- **Silvergate Bank** (crypto-friendly bank): voluntary liquidation March 2023.
- **Signature Bank** (crypto-friendly): closed by NY DFS March 12, 2023.
- **Silicon Valley Bank** (Circle's banking partner): closed March 10, 2023; USDC briefly de-pegged to $0.87 during weekend before FDIC resolution.

### Panic market signatures observed

- **Vol spike**: BTC realized 30-day vol >150% during Nov 2022.
- **Correlation → 1**: every "risk" asset moved together; BTC, ETH, alts, NFTs all crashed simultaneously.
- **Forced selling visible**: liquidation cascades on Coinglass — cumulative liquidations in November 2022 exceeded $30B aggregate.
- **Bid-side liquidity vanished briefly**: order book depth on major CEXes dropped to fractions of normal during the FTX collapse.
- **Bottom-fishing only after multiple capitulation events**: the November 21 low was tested December 30 (slightly higher); only after 2-3 months of basing did accumulation resume.

### Panic credit signatures observed

- **Funding markets seized**: funding deeply negative as shorts piled on; lending market borrow rates spiked.
- **Counterparty fear ("discredit")**: every CEX scrutinized; "proof of reserves" became standard demand.
- **Stablecoin supply collapse**: USDC fell from $43B (Nov 2022) to $32B (Dec 2022) on redemptions.
- **CEX withdrawal queues**: Binance briefly paused USDC withdrawals; Crypto.com saw withdrawal acceleration.

### Was there an LOLR action?

**No.** This is the load-bearing observation about crypto cycle dynamics.

- **Crypto-internal**: No LOLR materialized. CZ's Binance briefly considered buying FTX, walked away within 24 hours. Stablecoin issuers absorbed pressure but did not extend LOLR-style credit. No exchange consortium emerged.
- **TradFi external**: Indirect. The Fed's slow pivot through 2023 (signaling rate cuts in late 2023, finally cutting Sept 2024) restored macro liquidity. **But this is *secular liquidity recovery*, not *LOLR-style targeted intervention*. Crypto recovered without a rescue, on price exhaustion + macro tailwind.**

The FTX customer funds (~$8B) were permanent loss to the system at the time of bankruptcy filing. Subsequent recovery efforts brought back ~$15B+ via clawbacks and token-price appreciation through 2024, making creditors largely whole — but **at the time of panic-bottom, no one knew this**. The system absorbed the loss without a coordinated rescue.

This is consistent with [[foundation/sources/kindleberger-manias-panics-crashes|the Kindleberger source page LOLR section]] conclusion: crypto has no LOLR by design; panic terminates on price exhaustion or exchange halts.

## Cycle Summary

### Duration

| Phase | Approximate dates | Duration |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Displacement | Q1-Q2 2020 | 6 months (gradual unfolding) |
| Boom | Q3 2020 - Q4 2021 | 15 months |
| Euphoria | Q1 2021 - Nov 2021 | 11 months (with twin peaks Apr + Nov) |
| Distress | Q1 2022 - Q3 2022 | 9 months |
| Panic | Nov 2022 (intense) | ~1 month intense; bear continued through 2023 |
| **Total cycle to bottom** | **March 2020 - Nov 2022** | **~32 months** |

This is **within Kindleberger's 15-40 month bubble window** (his operational definition). Consistent.

### Magnitude

- **BTC peak-to-trough**: $69,044 → $15,476 = **−77.6%**
- **ETH peak-to-trough**: $4,891 → $880 (June 2022) = **−82.0%**
- **Aggregate market cap**: ~$3T → ~$830B = **−72%**
- **Comparable TradFi**: 2008 GFC S&P peak-to-trough −56%; 2000 dot-com NASDAQ peak-to-trough −78%; 1929 NYSE peak-to-trough −89% (over 3 years).

The crypto -77% is consistent with high-magnitude bubble cycles; **the absence of LOLR amplified the depth** vs comparable TradFi cycles like 2008. The dot-com -78% (also without targeted LOLR for tech specifically) is a closer analog than 2008 (which had aggressive Fed intervention).

### Stage-transition signals (retrospective)

| Transition | Forward signal that fired | Lag from signal to confirmation |
|-----------|---------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Displacement → Boom | Stablecoin supply began expanding; first DeFi yield products went live | ~3 months |
| Boom → Euphoria | "This time is different" rationalizations; retail app rankings; cocktail-party indicator | ~3 months |
| Euphoria → Distress | Macro pivot signaled Dec 2021; first peripheral failure (Anchor stress) Q1 2022 | ~3 months |
| Distress → Panic | Single-event trigger (CoinDesk FTT report Nov 2 → CZ tweet Nov 6 → bank run Nov 7) | ~5 days |
| Panic → Recovery | Liquidation cascade exhaustion + funding floor + on-chain panic-send peak | ~2 months |

The Distress→Panic transition was unusually *fast* (5 days) due to social-media propagation. The slower transitions (~3 months) are more typical.

## Lessons for the Wiki

### Confirms Kindleberger framework applicability to crypto

- All 5 stages identifiable with clear signatures.
- Duration within K's 15-40 month window.
- Magnitude consistent with high-amplitude / no-LOLR profile.
- "This time is different" rationalizations followed the textbook pattern.
- Insider/outsider flow imbalance was visible on-chain.
- Ponzi-finance composition was visible on-chain — Anchor 19.5%, 3AC stack, CeFi yield products.

### Confirms crypto's absence of LOLR

- No targeted intervention rescued FTX customer funds.
- Bottom was reached via price exhaustion, not announcement effect.
- Drawdown depth (-77%) consistent with TradFi non-LOLR cycles (dot-com).
- This will likely repeat in future cycles unless crypto develops self-LOLR mechanisms (no current candidate).

### Confirms on-chain visibility advantage

- Ponzi-finance positions (Anchor, 3AC, CeFi yields) were openly visible at the time.
- Whale-cohort distribution was on-chain.
- The "where does the cash come from?" question could be applied to most yield products in real time.
- Crypto traders have *more* visibility into the credit cycle than TradFi participants — and yet most still didn't apply the framework. The framework's value is conditional on *applying* it, not on availability of inputs.

### Open questions surfaced {#open-questions}
- **Were 2013, 2017, 2021 separate bubbles or one continuous bubble?** The 2020-2022 cycle inherited institutional structures and counterparty exposures from prior cycles (Mt. Gox claims still distributing; GBTC discount mechanic from 2017). Cycle continuity is a feature of crypto absent from TradFi.
- **Was 2024-? a new displacement (ETF approvals + Trump admin) or a continuation?** Both readings are defensible; live tracking via this framework is required.
- **Will the next cycle's panic be amplified or dampened by current institutional structure?** ETF presence may dampen (institutional flow as cushion); ETF presence may amplify (aligned-direction institutional flow as accelerant). Empirical question.

## Open Questions Specific to Future Cycles

1. **Will the 2024-? cycle have a distinct panic phase, or has it already had one (2025 Q1 ~$74K → $77K → $74K micro-correction)?** Live classification needed.
2. **What is the new cycle's Ponzi-finance signature?** (Restaking? Memecoin platforms? Real-World Assets tokenization?) Each new cycle invents new credit forms.
3. **Are the 2024-? displacement(s) genuinely real or already saturated?** ETF flows are real but the marginal incremental flow shrinks; if Boom is built on saturated displacement, Euphoria may be muted.
4. **What's the canonical "first peripheral failure" pattern for Stage 4 in the next cycle?** Will it be a CEX collapse again, or a different vector (algorithmic stable, restaking protocol, DeFi lending protocol)?

## Honest Caveats

- **This page is contemporaneous calibration, not retroactive ornament.** The signatures listed per stage are written *as if* they could be identified at the time. Future cycles will test whether the *signature templates* (not the specific values) generalize. The wiki should track ex-ante predictions vs ex-post outcomes.
- **Selection effects.** The 2020-2022 cycle is *one cycle* worth of evidence. Generalization to future cycles requires multi-cycle calibration. The 2017 cycle had similar but distinguishable signatures; cycles before 2017 may be too different in scale to compare cleanly.
- **AI training corpus bias.** Many of the cited statistics and dates above are from an AI training corpus that's heavy on 2020-2022 cycle coverage. Cross-cycle calibration via AI is unreliable. *Re-verify everything from primary sources for live trading decisions.*
- **The framework is descriptive, not predictive.** This page documents what happened; it doesn't tell you the next cycle will follow the same path. Stage labels are useful for pattern-recognition; they are not forecasts.

## Key Takeaways

- The 2020-2022 crypto cycle is a textbook 5-stage Kindleberger bubble: Displacement (COVID + DeFi + retail) → Boom (Q3 2020 - Q4 2021) → Euphoria (peak Nov 10, 2021) → Distress (Terra May 2022, 3AC June 2022) → Panic (FTX Nov 2022, BTC bottom $15.5K).
- Duration ~32 months — within Kindleberger's 15-40 month bubble window.
- Magnitude -77% BTC, -82% ETH — consistent with no-LOLR / dot-com profile.
- **Ponzi-finance signatures were visible contemporaneously**: Anchor 19.5%, 3AC GBTC/stETH/Luna stack, CeFi yield platforms. The "where does the cash come from?" diagnostic could be applied at the time.
- **No LOLR rescue**: cycle terminated on price exhaustion + macro tailwind, not central-bank intervention.
- Stage transitions had identifiable forward signals at ~3 month lag (except Distress→Panic which was 5 days).
- Crypto has *more* visibility into credit cycles than TradFi (on-chain data, public funding rates, transparent stablecoin supply) — but participants didn't always apply the framework even when inputs were available.
- This worked example is the wiki's first contemporaneous-style cycle classification; future cycles can be compared against this template.
