# AI Cycle Calibration 2024-2026 — Capex Buildout + Stage Classification

> **TL;DR**: Hyperscaler AI capex 2026 is $602-725B (36-64% YoY); GS projects $7.6T cumulative 2026-2031. Asset depreciation alone ($400B/yr at 20% rate) now exceeds combined hyperscaler profits. **Profitability and capex scale exceed dot-com**, but **monetization gap persists** (90% of firms report no AI productivity impact per NBER; 94% per McKinsey). Per [[foundation/market-wisdom/bubble-cycle-stages|Kindleberger framework]], current best read: **late-Boom transitioning to early-Euphoria**, with credible bullwhip-unwind risk in 2025-2026 if supply catches demand. Companion calibration anchor for the [[foundation/sources/hayes-essays|Hayes essays]] dollar-liquidity overlay and substrate for any AI-related investment thesis.

## Why This Page Exists

The 2026-05-07 research process session surfaced AI-infrastructure REITs (datacenter, power, cooling) as the **narrowest first investment thesis** at the intersection of two strong-CoC areas (AI/software + real estate). Building that thesis requires **calibrating where in the AI-buildout cycle we are** — Boom, Euphoria, Distress, or Panic per [[foundation/market-wisdom/bubble-cycle-stages|Kindleberger 5-stage]] framework — because:

- **Late-Boom**: capex demand persists, infrastructure underbuilt, pricing power strong → bullish for picks-and-shovels (REITs, power infrastructure, GPU suppliers)
- **Early-Euphoria**: capex demand still high but durability questions emerging → still bullish but with explicit tail-risk hedging
- **Distress / Panic**: bullwhip unwinds, hyperscaler capex cuts, mark-to-market lease pricing reverses → bearish for AI-infra-only plays

**This page is the calibration substrate** — not a recommendation. Research process sessions cite it; thesis pages reference it; future ingests refine it.

## Stage Classification (current best read, May 2026)

**Late-Boom transitioning to early-Euphoria.** Five-of-five Kindleberger stage signatures present, with stage-marker mix favoring late-Boom over Euphoria:

| Stage signature | Present? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Displacement** (real innovation as cycle trigger) | ✅ | ChatGPT Nov 2022 + GPT-4 Mar 2023 + multimodal models 2024-2025; genuine technological shift |
| **Boom** (credit + capital expansion to back the displacement) | ✅✅ | $602-725B hyperscaler capex 2026 (36-64% YoY); cumulative $7.6T projected 2026-2031 (GS Global Institute) |
| **Euphoria** (rationalizations: "this time is different") | ⚠️ partial | "AI productivity supercycle" narrative dominant; but **adoption gap is wiki-encoded** (NBER: 90% of firms report no AI productivity impact; McKinsey: 94% no significant value despite near-universal adoption) |
| **Distress** (cracks visible to careful observers) | ⚠️ early | (a) **depreciation > profits**: $400B/yr at 20% rate exceeds combined hyperscaler net income; (b) **asset-life assumption**: 5-6 years for GPUs/servers — if shorter, ROI breaks; (c) **bullwhip risk**: 2025-2026 inflection point per Gad Allon analysis |
| **Panic** (sell-everything) | ❌ | Not present; AI capex still accelerating, pricing power intact |

**Implication for stage call**: cracks are visible (depreciation > profits, monetization gap, asset-life uncertainty) but Boom-side signatures dominate (capex acceleration, pricing power, infrastructure constrained). Most consistent label: **late-Boom with early-Distress stress overlay** — analogous to the [[foundation/macro/btc-regime-taxonomy-2020-2025|May 4 BTC regime call]] structure (mixed late-Boom + Distress-stress).

## Key Cycle Metrics (track these going forward)

### Capex Scale

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Big 5 hyperscaler capex (Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Oracle) | ~$200B | ~$410B | $602-725B | Introl / Futurum / CreditSights |
| YoY growth | — | ~105% | ~36-64% | derived |
| % targeting AI specifically | ~50% | ~65% | ~75% ($450B+) | Introl |
| GS cumulative AI capex 2026-2031 (projected) | — | — | $7.6T | Goldman Sachs |
| Annual run rate by 2031 (projected) | — | — | $1.6T | Goldman Sachs |

**Calibration anchor**: 2026 AI capex is roughly **3% of US GDP** ($720B / ~$30T) — comparable to housing capex peak in 2006-2007 (~3.5%). Not a verdict, just a scale anchor for "this is meaningful at the macro level."

### Depreciation Reality Check

- **GPU/server useful life assumption**: 5-6 years (hyperscaler accounting)
- **Annual depreciation expense**: ~$400B at 20%/yr rate
- **vs combined hyperscaler net income**: depreciation now exceeds it
- **Tail risk**: if AI hardware obsoletes faster (3-4 years instead of 5-6), required reinvestment rate climbs sharply; ROI assumptions break

This is the **single most important "early-Distress" signature** to track. Any hyperscaler that revises depreciation downward (shorter useful life) is implicitly admitting capex was too aggressive relative to monetization timeline.

### Monetization Gap

- **NBER (Feb 2026)**: 90% of firms report no AI impact on workplace productivity
- **McKinsey 2025 survey**: 94% of companies report no "significant" value from AI investments
- **Executive expectation**: 1.4% productivity uplift projected
- **Reality**: not yet measurable in BLS productivity data
- **Capex vs revenue**: hyperscaler AI capex outpaces AI-product revenue by ~10x at current run rates

**Honest read**: AI is generating real revenue (NVDA, hyperscaler AI services) but **end-customer productivity is not yet capturing the capex investment**. This is the gap that closes (Boom continues / Euphoria validated) or doesn't (Distress / Panic crystallizes). Closure timeline is the open question.

### Profitability vs Dot-Com (key difference)

| Metric | Dot-com peak (Mar 2000) | AI cycle (early 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Nasdaq-100 forward P/E | ~60× | ~26-30× |
| S&P 500 forward P/E | ~30× | ~23× (most stretched since dot-com) |
| Largest tech firm trailing earnings | Cisco: ~$2.7B net income | NVIDIA: ~$99B TTM net income, 53% net margin |
| Largest tech firm market cap | Cisco: $555B (Mar 2000 peak) | NVIDIA: $4.3T (Feb 2026) |
| Are leaders profitable? | Mostly no (dot-coms) | Yes (NVDA + hyperscalers + Mag-7) |
| Is the buildout cash-financed? | Mostly debt + IPO equity | Mostly hyperscaler operating cash flow |

**This is the load-bearing difference from dot-com**: today's AI buildout is cash-financed by profitable incumbents, not debt-financed by speculative new entrants. This **doesn't make AI bubble-proof**, but it changes the bust mechanics — a dot-com-style bankruptcy cascade is structurally harder when the buildout is funded from operating cash flow.

## Comparison to Prior Cycles

### Dot-Com (1995-2000-2002)

**Similarities**:
- Real technological displacement (internet) → genuine cycle trigger
- Capex acceleration far ahead of revenue (telecom buildout)
- "This time is different" narrative dominant near peak
- Concentration risk (5-7 stocks driving index returns)

**Differences**:
- Today's leaders are profitable (see table above)
- Today's buildout is cash-financed, not debt/IPO-financed
- AI revenue is growing (NVDA, AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, etc.), even if customer-side ROI isn't measurable yet
- Dot-com peak P/E ~2× current; absolute valuations less stretched

**Honest assessment**: AI may bubble (and probably has bubble characteristics in some segments — speculative AI-startup VC, "AI" branded SPACs, individual high-multiple names) but **the macro structural risk of a dot-com-style cascade is lower** because the financing mechanics differ.

### Telecom Buildout (1996-2002)

**Critical analogy that's underused**: telecom fiber buildout 1996-2002 saw similar capex-ahead-of-demand dynamics. WorldCom, Global Crossing, Qwest, JDSU all built massive fiber infrastructure on the assumption demand would catch up. It did — but **5-10 years later**, after the bust. Fiber assets bought at $0.05 on the dollar in 2003-2005 paid spectacularly.

**Lesson for AI cycle**: even if capex outruns demand and bullwhip-unwinds in 2025-2027, **the underlying infrastructure is not destroyed** — datacenters, GPUs (eventually obsoleted), fiber connections, power capacity all retain value. The question is **which assets retain value through a downturn** (datacenter shells, power capacity = high; specific GPU generations = low) and **who owns them at the trough** (hyperscalers / REITs / distressed-asset buyers).

### Crypto 2020-2022 Cycle ([[foundation/macro/crypto-2020-2022-cycle]])

Less directly analogous, but the **5-stage Kindleberger fit** is similar: real technological displacement → capex/capital expansion → euphoria narratives → distress → panic. 2026 AI is in the cap-expansion phase; crypto reached panic by Nov 2022. **Cycle compression in crypto** (4 years peak-to-trough) is faster than equity cycles (10-15 years); AI cycle pacing likely sits between (5-8 years).

## Decision-Relevant Signals (track for stage transition)

**Boom → Euphoria transition signatures** (watch list):
- AI-named SPACs / direct-listing surge
- "Productivity supercycle" narrative becoming consensus (currently disputed)
- Capex guidance revisions UP (currently happening Q1 2026)
- Multiple expansion (P/E expansion) without earnings revision
- Concentration risk peaks (currently ~30% S&P weight in Mag-7)

**Euphoria → Distress transition signatures** (watch list):
- **Hyperscaler depreciation policy change** (shorter useful life = first crack)
- **Capex guidance revision DOWN** at any major hyperscaler (currently none — Q1 2026 was up)
- **NVIDIA gross margin compression** from current 70%+ levels (early supply-catch-up signal)
- **Datacenter REIT mark-to-market lease growth slowing** (currently 30-50% above expiring; deceleration would signal supply catching demand)
- **AI startup valuation marks DOWN** in late-stage VC rounds
- **End-customer ROI study showing measurable productivity** (positive scenario — closes the monetization gap → continues cycle; absence sustains the gap → distress signature)

**Distress → Panic transition** (worst case):
- Hyperscaler cancels major datacenter project
- Public mark-down of GPU inventory
- Sovereign-fund reversal in AI infrastructure (sovereign was a 2024-2025 demand pillar)
- Power-grid bottleneck breaks (positive: unlocks more capacity; negative: forces capex reallocation)

## Implications for Asset Classes

### AI-Infrastructure REITs (the research process target sector)

**Bull case** (consistent with current late-Boom call):
- Power is the binding constraint — datacenter REIT vacancy <3%
- Mark-to-market lease pricing 30-50% above expiring leases
- Multi-year lease structure means pricing power compounds (3-5 year mark-to-market reset)
- Hyperscaler capex cycle is multi-year (5-8 years to capacity), not multi-quarter

**Bear case** (consistent with early-Distress overlay):
- If hyperscalers slow capex, datacenter REITs face oversupply on a 2-3 year lag
- Power-grid bottleneck is real — but if it resolves, supply can outrun demand
- Cap-rate expansion in a recession would compress NAV regardless of operating performance

See [[foundation/methodology/reit-evaluation-methodology]] for the framework to evaluate these REITs.

### NVIDIA / GPU Suppliers

- Concentrated risk: NVDA is ~70%+ of incremental GPU supply
- Margin >50% net is unusual durability; sustainable only if AI demand stays infrastructure-constrained (i.e., if monetization gap closes)
- Bullwhip risk: 2025-2026 supply catch-up could compress margins sharply

### Hyperscalers

- Profitable (cash-financed buildout), but depreciation now > profits — operating leverage works in both directions
- Watch depreciation policy as first-distress-signature

### Power Infrastructure

- **Most underbuilt asset class in the cycle**: US grid + nuclear + natural gas peakers all face multi-year permitting + construction lead times
- Even if AI cycle slows, power-buildout duration > AI-cycle volatility window
- Companion to AI-infra REIT thesis on the bull-side; potentially separable thesis

### Long-duration assets (REITs, utilities)

- Late-Boom phase typically late-cycle for rate-sensitive long-duration plays
- 10y Treasury ~4.40% (May 2026) is elevated — REIT/utility valuation pressure from rates is real
- Watch Fed pivot timing (per [[foundation/macro/btc-regime-taxonomy-2020-2025|2026-Q2 regime markers]]); current pricing: no cuts for remainder of 2026

## Crypto-Specific Translation

Crypto's relationship to the AI cycle is **second-order** (not direct):

- AI capex creates inflationary pressure (power demand → energy prices → real-rate pressure on long-duration risk assets including BTC)
- AI corporate-treasury narrative: BTC adopted as "scarce digital infrastructure" alongside compute infrastructure (echoes 2020-2021 thesis but with fresh institutional cohort post-ETF)
- Hayes (2026-05-08 PM-2 ingest) "$HYPE Man" + "This Is Fine" essays explicitly link AI-displacement → bank-credit cascade speculation as a forward scenario (treat as regime-watch input, not validated mechanism per [[foundation/methodology/hayes-essays-ingest|Hayes ingest discipline]])

**Honest read**: AI cycle does NOT directly drive BTC short-term, but it shapes the macro liquidity regime (power-driven inflation risk + capex-driven productivity-or-not question) that affects BTC long-term.

## Honest Caveats / Open Questions

- **Stage call is current-best, not certain.** The mix of Boom signatures (capex acceleration, pricing power) + early-Distress signatures (depreciation > profits, monetization gap) is consistent with multiple histories — telecom 1999, dot-com 1999, oil 1980. Each had different bust dynamics. Cross-comparison is suggestive, not deterministic.
- **Bullwhip-unwind timing is unknown.** Gad Allon's 2025-2026 inflection-point call is one analysis; consensus is more diffuse. Signal-checking depends on watching the listed transition signatures.
- **Monetization gap closure is the swing variable.** If end-customer AI productivity becomes measurable in 2026-2027, cycle continues (Boom → Euphoria → eventual late-Euphoria peak). If gap persists into 2028, capex outruns demand and bust mechanics activate.
- **Post-IRA / political risk**: any policy change affecting hyperscaler tax treatment or AI-export controls could shift capex trajectory mid-cycle.
- **Sovereign-fund involvement**: Saudi PIF, ADIA, GIC, Norwegian SWF all increasing AI-infra exposure 2024-2025. This is a **stabilizing demand layer in the buildout but a tail risk if reversed** — unlike retail or VC capital, sovereign capital can be politically reversed.
- **Capex / monetization gap rests on linear extrapolation**. AI productivity could be S-curve (slow start, then breakout) — in which case current "no productivity impact" reading would be early-S-curve and resolve favorably. Or it could be permanent gap (the cynical take). Both are plausible.
